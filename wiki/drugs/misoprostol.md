## Clinical Definition

Misoprostol is a synthetic prostaglandin E1 analog with uterotonic, cervical ripening, and gastrointestinal cytoprotective properties (1A). It is a cornerstone drug in obstetrics for medical abortion, labor induction, and postpartum hemorrhage management.

## Indications

- Medical abortion (first and second trimester) (1A)
- Cervical ripening and labor induction (1A)
- Prevention and treatment of postpartum hemorrhage (1A)
- First-trimester incomplete abortion management (1A)
- Prevention of NSAID-induced gastric ulcers (non-obstetric)

## Dosing / Technique

**Postpartum hemorrhage (uterine atony):**
- 800–1000 mcg rectally or 600 mcg sublingually (off-label but widely used) (1A)
- WHO recommends 800 mcg sublingually when IV oxytocin unavailable

**Medical abortion:**
- 800 mcg buccal/sublingual/vaginal 24–48 hours after mifepristone (1A)

**Labor induction:**
- 25 mcg vaginally every 4–6 hours (low-dose protocol preferred in term inductions) (1A)

## Evidence for Efficacy

- At least as effective as oxytocin for PPH in settings without IV access (1A — Cochrane)
- First-line for medical abortion with mifepristone (1A)
- Effective for cervical ripening with reduced need for cesarean vs dinoprostone in some studies (1A)

## Contraindications and Precautions

- Prior uterine scar (relative — use lower doses, monitor for rupture in VBAC candidates)
- Avoid in women desiring to continue pregnancy (teratogenic in first trimester)
- Cardiovascular disease (caution with high doses)

## Adverse Effects

- Shivering, fever, diarrhea (common)
- Uterine tachysystole
- Nausea, vomiting
- Rare: uterine rupture (especially with prior cesarean and high doses)

## Pregnancy / Lactation

- **Pregnancy:** Category X if used for abortion; otherwise used therapeutically
- **Lactation:** Compatible; minimal transfer to breast milk
