## Clinical Definition

Labetalol is a combined non-selective β-blocker and selective α₁-blocker used as first-line oral and IV antihypertensive in pregnancy, particularly for acute severe hypertension in preeclampsia (1B).

## Indications

- Acute severe hypertension in pregnancy (BP ≥160/110) (1B)
- Chronic hypertension in pregnancy (oral maintenance)
- Preeclampsia with severe features requiring blood pressure control

## Dosing / Technique

**Acute severe hypertension (IV):**
- 20 mg IV over 2 minutes; double every 10 minutes (40 mg, 80 mg) to max 300 mg
- Alternative: 1–2 mg/min continuous infusion

**Chronic maintenance (oral):**
- 100–200 mg PO twice daily; titrate to max 2400 mg/day

## Evidence for Efficacy

- Rapid onset (2–5 minutes IV) with peak at 5–15 minutes (1B)
- Does not reduce uteroplacental blood flow at usual doses (1B)
- Comparable efficacy to hydralazine for acute control; fewer maternal side effects than hydralazine in some studies (1B)

## Contraindications and Precautions

- Asthma / COPD with bronchospasm (non-selective β-blockade)
- Bradycardia, heart block, decompensated heart failure
- Avoid in patients with cocaine use (unopposed α-stimulation worsens hypertension)
- Neonatal: may cause transient hypoglycemia and bradycardia; monitor newborn for 24–48 hours

## Adverse Effects

- Maternal: dizziness, fatigue, nausea, scalp tingling (unique to labetalol)
- Bradycardia, hypotension
- Hepatotoxicity (rare idiosyncratic reaction)
- Neonatal: hypoglycemia, bradycardia, hypotonia

## Pregnancy / Lactation

- **Pregnancy:** Preferred first-line antihypertensive in pregnancy per ACOG (1B)
- **Lactation:** Compatible; minimal transfer to breast milk
