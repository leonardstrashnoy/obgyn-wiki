## Clinical Definition

Methyldopa is a centrally acting alpha-2 adrenergic agonist that reduces sympathetic outflow, lowering blood pressure. It has been the most extensively studied antihypertensive in pregnancy with long-term safety data (1B).

## Indications

- Chronic hypertension in pregnancy
- Mild-moderate hypertension without severe features
- Long-standing maintenance therapy

## Dosing / Technique

- 250 mg PO BID–TID initially; titrate to max 3 g/day
- Onset: 4–6 hours; peak: 6–8 hours

## Evidence for Efficacy

- Extensive safety data in pregnancy; no teratogenicity
- Less effective than labetalol/nifecipine for acute control (slower onset)
- First-line for chronic maintenance, not acute severe hypertension

## Adverse Effects

- Sedation, fatigue, depression
- Hepatotoxicity (rare; check LFTs)
- Hemolytic anemia (Coombs-positive, usually benign)
- Dry mouth, nasal congestion

## Pregnancy / Lactation

- **Pregnancy:** Safe; first-line for chronic maintenance (1B)
- **Lactation:** Compatible; minimal transfer

## Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence Level | Basis |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Long-term safety established in pregnancy | 1B | Decades of use + follow-up studies |
| Not for acute severe hypertension | 2B | Slow onset |
