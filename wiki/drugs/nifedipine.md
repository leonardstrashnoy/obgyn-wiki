## Clinical Definition

Nifedipine is a dihydropyridine calcium channel blocker used as second-line antihypertensive in pregnancy and as a tocolytic agent for acute preterm labor suppression (1B).

## Indications

- Chronic and acute hypertension in pregnancy (oral or SL) (1B)
- Acute tocolysis (short-term suppression of preterm labor) (1B)
- Raynaud phenomenon (non-obstetric)

## Dosing / Technique

**Antihypertensive (acute):**
- 10 mg PO; repeat every 20 minutes × 2 if needed; then 10–20 mg every 4–6 hours

**Tocolysis:**
- Immediate-release: 10–20 mg PO every 4–6 hours for 48–72 hours
- Avoid sublingual (unpredictable absorption, hypotension risk)

## Evidence for Efficacy

- Effective for acute blood pressure reduction in pregnancy; comparable to labetalol/hydralazine (1B)
- Effective tocolytic with fewer maternal side effects than terbutaline or magnesium sulfate for short-term suppression (1B — Cochrane)
- Does not adversely affect fetal Doppler or biophysical profile at tocolytic doses (1B)

## Contraindications and Precautions

- Do NOT administer with magnesium sulfate simultaneously (neuromuscular blockade, severe hypotension)
- Avoid in patients with hypotension, aortic stenosis, or cardiogenic shock
- CYP3A4 interactions (contraindicated with strong inhibitors like ketoconazole, macrolides)

## Adverse Effects

- Headache, flushing, dizziness (common, usually transient)
- Peripheral edema
- Tachycardia, palpitations
- Reflex hypotension (rare with oral dosing)
- Maternal pulmonary edema (rare, case reports with tocolysis)

## Pregnancy / Lactation

- **Pregnancy:** Compatible; no teratogenicity at usual doses
- **Lactation:** Compatible; minimal transfer; consider maternal side effects
