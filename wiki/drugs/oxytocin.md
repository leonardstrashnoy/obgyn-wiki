## Clinical Definition

Oxytocin is a synthetic nonapeptide identical to the posterior pituitary hormone. It is the first-line uterotonic for prevention and treatment of postpartum hemorrhage and for labor induction/augmentation (1A).

## Indications

- Prevention of postpartum hemorrhage (active management of third stage) (1A)
- Treatment of uterine atony after vaginal or cesarean delivery (1A)
- Labor induction and augmentation (1A)
- Adjunctive therapy in incomplete abortion (2B)

## Dosing / Technique

**PPH prevention (active management third stage):**
- 10 IU IM or 5–10 IU IV slow push after delivery of anterior shoulder or immediately after delivery (1A)

**PPH treatment (uterine atony):**
- 10–40 IU in 1 L crystalloid IV infusion (titrate to uterine tone); avoid IV push of undiluted oxytocin (hypotension, arrhythmia)
- Higher concentrations up to 80 IU/L if refractory

**Labor induction:**
- Low-dose protocol: 0.5–2 mU/min, increase 1–2 mU/min every 30–40 min
- High-dose protocol: 6 mU/min, increase 3–6 mU/min every 15 min (faster but more tachysystole)

## Evidence for Efficacy

- Reduces PPH (>500 mL blood loss) by ~50% when used prophylactically vs expectant management (1A — Cochrane)
- More effective than ergometrine for PPH prevention with fewer side effects (1A)
- Active management (oxytocin + controlled cord traction) reduces severe PPH (>1000 mL) and transfusion need (1A)

## Contraindications and Precautions

- Avoid rapid IV bolus (hypotension, tachycardia, arrhythmia)
- Caution in cardiac disease
- Uterine hyperstimulation/tachysystole — reduce or discontinue if fetal heart rate concerns
- Placental abruption with fetal distress (relative — balance delivery urgency)

## Adverse Effects

- Uterine tachysystole, hypertonus
- Hypotension (with rapid IV bolus)
- Water intoxication / hyponatremia (rare, with prolonged high-dose infusion in hypotonic solutions)
- Nausea, vomiting (less common than with ergot alkaloids)

## Pregnancy / Lactation

- **Pregnancy:** Used therapeutically in labor and delivery; no teratogenicity
- **Lactation:** Endogenous oxytocin mediates milk ejection reflex; synthetic form compatible
