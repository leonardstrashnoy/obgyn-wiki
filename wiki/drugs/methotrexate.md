## Clinical Definition

Methotrexate is a folic acid antagonist (dihydrofolate reductase inhibitor) used as first-line medical therapy for hemodynamically stable ectopic pregnancy and for medical abortion regimens (2B).

## Indications

- Unruptured ectopic pregnancy with β-hCG < 5000 IU/L and no fetal cardiac activity (2B)
- Medical abortion in combination with mifepristone (1B)
- Gestational trophoblastic disease (2B)
- Rheumatoid arthritis / psoriasis (non-obstetric)

## Dosing / Technique

**Ectopic pregnancy:**
- Single-dose: 50 mg/m² IM; follow β-hCG at days 4 and 7; repeat if plateau or rise < 15% decline
- Multi-dose: 1 mg/kg IM alternating with leucovorin 0.1 mg/kg PO on days 1, 3, 5, 7

**Medical abortion:**
- 50 mg PO day 1 with mifepristone 200 mg; then misoprostol 800 mcg buccally 24–48h later (1B)

## Evidence for Efficacy

- Success rate for ectopic: 65–95% depending on initial β-hCG and protocol (2B)
- Higher success with lower initial β-hCG and smaller mass (2B)
- No significant difference in future fertility between methotrexate and surgery (2B)

## Contraindications and Precautions

- Breastfeeding (contraindicated)
- Immunodeficiency, active infection, severe anemia
- Hepatic/renal/hematologic dysfunction
- Hemodynamically unstable patient or ruptured ectopic (surgical emergency)
- Fetal cardiac activity or β-hCG > 5000 IU/L (lower success, consider surgery) (2B)

## Adverse Effects

- Stomatitis, nausea, vomiting
- Transaminitis
- Bone marrow suppression (rare at obstetric doses)
- Dermatitis, photosensitivity
- Teratogenic — avoid in desired intrauterine pregnancy

## Pregnancy / Lactation

- **Pregnancy category X** — teratogenic; used to terminate ectopic pregnancies
- **Lactation:** Contraindicated; interrupt breastfeeding during treatment
