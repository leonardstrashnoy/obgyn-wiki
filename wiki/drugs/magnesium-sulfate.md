## Clinical Definition

Magnesium sulfate is an inorganic salt with multiple obstetric indications: eclampsia seizure prophylaxis/treatment, fetal neuroprotection before anticipated preterm delivery, and short-term tocolysis (1A).

## Indications

- **Eclampsia prophylaxis/treatment:** First-line anticonvulsant in severe preeclampsia (1A)
- **Fetal neuroprotection:** Before anticipated preterm birth < 32 weeks (1A — reduces cerebral palsy risk)
- **Short-term tocolysis:** 48 hours to allow corticosteroid administration (1B)

## Dosing / Technique

**Eclampsia / severe preeclampsia:**
- Loading: 4–6 g IV over 15–20 minutes
- Maintenance: 1–2 g/hour continuous IV infusion
- Recurrent seizure: 2 g IV bolus

**Fetal neuroprotection:**
- Loading: 4 g IV over 30 minutes
- Maintenance: 1 g/hour until delivery or 24 hours

## Evidence for Efficacy

- MAGPIE trial: reduces eclampsia risk by 50% in severe preeclampsia (1A)
- BEAM trial and meta-analysis: reduces moderate-severe cerebral palsy in infants born < 32 weeks when given antenatally (1A)
- Effective short-term tocolysis but not superior to other agents for prolonging pregnancy beyond 48 hours (1B)

## Contraindications and Precautions

- Myasthenia gravis (absolute)
- Severe renal failure (accumulation risk; reduce dose, monitor levels)
- Cardiac disease with conduction abnormalities
- Monitor: deep tendon reflexes, respiratory rate (>12/min), urine output (>25–30 mL/h)
- Toxicity: loss of reflexes at 8–10 mg/dL, respiratory depression at 10–12 mg/dL, cardiac arrest > 12.5 mg/dL
- Antidote: 10% calcium gluconate 10 mL IV (reverses magnesium toxicity)

## Adverse Effects

- Flushing, warmth, nausea
- Headache, blurred vision
- Loss of deep tendon reflexes (early toxicity sign)
- Respiratory depression, cardiac arrest (severe toxicity)
- Neonatal: transient hypotonia, respiratory depression if maternal toxicity

## Pregnancy / Lactation

- **Pregnancy:** Standard of care for indicated conditions; no teratogenicity
- **Lactation:** Compatible; magnesium concentration in milk low; infant absorption minimal
