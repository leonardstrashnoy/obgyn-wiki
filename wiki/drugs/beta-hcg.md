## Clinical Definition

Human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG) is a glycoprotein hormone produced by syncytiotrophoblast. The beta subunit (β-hCG) is measured in serum and urine for pregnancy diagnosis, dating, and monitoring of ectopic pregnancy and gestational trophoblastic disease (1A).

## Physiology

- Detectable 8–11 days after conception
- Doubling time: ~48 hours in early normal pregnancy (1.9–2.7 days)
- Peak: 8–11 weeks, then declines
- Plateau or slow rise suggests ectopic pregnancy or failed pregnancy

## Clinical Uses

- **Pregnancy confirmation:** Urine ≥ 25 IU/L; serum ≥ 5 IU/L
- **Ectopic pregnancy:** < 66% rise in 48 hours suggests ectopic or failed IUP (2B)
- **Trophoblastic disease:** Very high levels (> 100,000); used to monitor treatment response
- **Down syndrome screening:** Low free β-hCG + low PAPP-A
