## Clinical Definition

Metformin is a biguanide oral hypoglycemic agent that reduces hepatic glucose production and improves peripheral insulin sensitivity. It is increasingly used in pregnancy for PCOS and gestational diabetes (1B).

## Indications

- **PCOS:** Restores ovulation, reduces metabolic syndrome
- **Gestational diabetes:** Alternative or adjunct to insulin (1B)
- **Type 2 diabetes:** First-line in non-pregnant patients

## Evidence in Pregnancy

- Crosses placenta; long-term offspring data reassuring but limited
- MiTy trial: metformin + insulin vs insulin alone in GDM — no difference in neonatal outcomes; less maternal weight gain and hypoglycemia with metformin (1B)
- May reduce GDM incidence in PCOS (contested) (1B)

## Contraindications

- Renal impairment (eGFR < 30)
- Hepatic impairment
- Ketoacidosis
- Hypoxic states
- Iodinated contrast procedures (hold 48 hours)

## Adverse Effects

- GI upset (nausea, diarrhea, metallic taste)
- Vitamin B12 deficiency (long-term)
- Lactic acidosis (rare, ~3 per 100,000 patient-years)

## Pregnancy / Lactation

- **Pregnancy:** Safe; crosses placenta but no teratogenicity; may reduce insulin needs
- **Lactation:** Compatible; low levels in breast milk
