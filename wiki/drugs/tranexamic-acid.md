## Clinical Definition

Tranexamic acid (TXA) is a synthetic lysine analog that inhibits plasminogen activation and fibrinolysis. It is the only pharmacologic agent proven to reduce mortality in postpartum hemorrhage when added to uterotonics (1A).

## Indications

- Postpartum hemorrhage with ongoing bleeding despite uterotonics (1A)
- Prophylaxis in cesarean delivery (contested; some RCTs show benefit, others neutral) (1B)
- Trauma, surgery, and menorrhagia (non-obstetric)

## Dosing / Technique

**Postpartum hemorrhage (WOMAN trial protocol):**
- 1 g IV over 10 minutes as soon as possible after diagnosis
- Repeat 1 g IV if bleeding continues after 30 minutes or restarts within 24 hours
- Maximum 2 g total

## Evidence for Efficacy

- WOMAN trial (n=20,060): TXA reduced death from bleeding by ~30% when given within 3 hours of diagnosis (RR 0.81, 95% CI 0.65–1.00); strongest benefit if given within 1 hour (1A)
- No benefit for prophylaxis in routine vaginal delivery (1A)
- Mixed evidence for cesarean prophylaxis (1B)

## Contraindications and Precautions

- Active intravascular clotting / DIC (relative — fibrinolysis may be compensatory)
- History of venous thromboembolism (theoretical concern; not borne out in trial data)
- Renal impairment (reduce dose if CrCl < 30)
- Avoid intrathecal/epidural injection

## Adverse Effects

- Nausea, vomiting
- Visual disturbances (transient, reversible)
- Seizures (rare, usually with overdose or neuraxial administration)
- Thromboembolic events: not increased in RCT data despite theoretical concern

## Pregnancy / Lactation

- **Pregnancy:** Used only postpartum; does not cross placenta in clinically relevant amounts after delivery
- **Lactation:** Compatible; low oral bioavailability in infant
