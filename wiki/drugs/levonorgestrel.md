# Levonorgestrel

**Levonorgestrel (LNG)** is a second-generation progestin widely used in contraception (oral emergency contraception, IUDs, implants).

## Mechanism of Action

- **Emergency contraception**: Primarily inhibition or delay of ovulation; does not disrupt established pregnancy
- **LNG-IUD**: Local endometrial effects, thick cervical mucus, variable ovulation suppression

## Uses

1. **Emergency contraception** — 1.5 mg single dose
2. **LNG-IUD** (Mirena, Kyleena, Skyla) — 3-8 year intrauterine contraception
3. **Implant** (Nexplanon) — 3-year subdermal progestin-only contraception

## Efficacy

| Formulation | Failure Rate |
|-------------|--------------|
| Emergency (1.5 mg) | 1-2% (varies by cycle timing) |
| LNG-IUD | 0.1-0.4% |
| Implant | 0.1% |

## Contraindications

**LNG-IUD only**: Active pelvic infection, unexplained uterine bleeding, uterine anomaly preventing placement

## Side Effects

- **Emergency**: Nausea, vomiting, irregular bleeding, headache
- **IUD**: Irregular bleeding initially (resolves), amenorrhea (20% at 1 year), expulsion (2-5%)
- **Implant**: Irregular bleeding (most common reason for discontinuation)

## Evidence Level

1A (extensive RCT data)
