## Clinical Definition

Carboprost tromethamine (Hemabate®) is a 15-methyl prostaglandin F2α analog with potent and sustained uterotonic activity. It is a third-line agent for postpartum hemorrhage due to uterine atony (2B).

## Indications

- Refractory postpartum hemorrhage from uterine atony after failure of oxytocin and ergot alkaloids (2B)
- Off-label: postpartum hemorrhage prophylaxis in high-risk settings (4)

## Dosing / Technique

- 250 mcg (1 mL) IM; may repeat every 15–90 minutes
- Maximum total dose: 2 mg (8 doses)

## Evidence for Efficacy

- 75–88% success rate in controlling refractory uterine atony (2B)
- Often avoids need for surgical/interventional radiology in refractory cases
- Comparable efficacy to misoprostol in some settings (2B)

## Contraindications and Precautions

- **Asthma:** Relative contraindication (bronchospasm risk via PGF2α)
- Cardiovascular, hepatic, renal disease (caution)
- Avoid in acute pelvic inflammatory disease

## Adverse Effects

- Diarrhea (most common, ~80%)
- Nausea, vomiting
- Fever, flushing
- Bronchospasm (asthmatics)
- Hypertension (less than ergot alkaloids)

## Pregnancy / Lactation

- **Pregnancy:** Used only postpartum; no fetal concerns after delivery
- **Lactation:** Unknown effects; use clinical judgment
