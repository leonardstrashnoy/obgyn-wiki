## Clinical Definition

Methylergonovine (methylergometrine) is a semisynthetic ergot alkaloid with potent uterotonic activity used as second-line therapy for postpartum hemorrhage due to uterine atony (1A).

## Indications

- Treatment of postpartum hemorrhage from uterine atony when oxytocin insufficient (1A)
- Prevention of postpartum hemorrhage (less commonly used due to side effects)

## Dosing / Technique

- 0.2 mg IM every 2–4 hours (maximum 5 doses)
- Avoid IV route due to severe hypertension and cerebrovascular events

## Evidence for Efficacy

- Highly effective for uterine atony refractory to oxytocin (1A)
- Often used in combination with oxytocin as part of "uterotonics bundle"

## Contraindications and Precautions

- **Hypertension / preeclampsia:** Absolute contraindication (can precipitate hypertensive crisis, stroke)
- Cardiovascular disease
- Peripheral vascular disease
- Hepatic/renal impairment
- Pregnancy (contraindicated due to vasoconstriction and uterine tetany)

## Adverse Effects

- Hypertension (most significant risk)
- Nausea, vomiting
- Headache, dizziness
- Peripheral vasoconstriction / gangrene (rare, with prolonged use)

## Pregnancy / Lactation

- **Pregnancy:** Contraindicated
- **Lactation:** Compatible at usual doses; may suppress lactation at high/prolonged doses
