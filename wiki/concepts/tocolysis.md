## Clinical Definition

Tocolysis is pharmacologic suppression of uterine contractions to delay preterm delivery, typically for a 48-hour window to allow antenatal corticosteroid administration and maternal transport to a tertiary center (1B).

## Agents

| Agent | Mechanism | Evidence | Notes |
|-------|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Nifedipine** | Ca²⁺ channel blocker | 1B | Preferred first-line (Cochrane); fewer side effects |
| **Indomethacin** | COX inhibitor | 1B | Effective; limit to <32 weeks (ductus risk) |
| **Magnesium sulfate** | Ca²⁺ antagonism | 1B | Effective short-term; also neuroprotective |
| **Terbutaline** | β₂-agonist | 1B | Effective but maternal side effects; no longer first-line |
| **Atosiban** | Oxytocin antagonist | 1B | Less effective; not available in US |

## Contraindications

- Chorioamnionitis (delivery indicated)
- Fetal distress / nonreassuring status
- Severe preeclampsia or eclampsia
- Fetal demise / lethal anomaly
- Advanced cervical dilation (often > 4–6 cm futile)

## Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence Level | Basis |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| 48-hour window for steroids is primary goal | 1B | ACOG + Cochrane |
| Nifedipine preferred first-line | 1B | Cochrane |

| Contraindicated in chorioamnionitis | 1B | Expert consensus |
