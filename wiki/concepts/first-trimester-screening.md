## Clinical Definition

First trimester screening is a combined approach using maternal serum biochemistry and ultrasound to assess risk for fetal aneuploidy (primarily trisomy 21, 18, 13) and structural anomalies (1A).

## Components

- **Nuchal translucency (NT):** 11–14 weeks; ≥ 3 mm abnormal
- **Serum markers:** PAPP-A, free β-hCG
- **Combined detection rate:** 85–90% for trisomy 21 at 5% FPR (1A)

## Alternatives

- **Cell-free DNA (NIPT):** >99% sensitivity for T21, T18, T13; >99% NPV (1A)
- **Contingent screening:** FTS first, high-risk referred to NIPT (cost-effective)
