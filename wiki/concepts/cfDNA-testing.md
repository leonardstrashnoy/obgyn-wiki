## Clinical Definition

Non-invasive prenatal testing (NIPT) analyzes cell-free DNA from the placental trophoblast circulating in maternal blood. It is the most sensitive screening test for fetal aneuploidy (1A).

## Performance

- **Trisomy 21:** >99% sensitivity, >99% NPV, FPR ~0.1% (1A)
- **Trisomy 18, 13:** Slightly lower sensitivity (~95–99%)
- **Sex chromosome aneuploidies:** Moderate PPV (~50–70%)
- **Microdeletions:** Lower PPV; not routinely recommended by ACOG

## Limitations

- Does not screen for structural anomalies
- False positive in confined placental mosaicism, vanishing twin, maternal malignancy, maternal mosaicism
- Low fetal fraction (<4%) → test failure; higher failure rate in obesity

## Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence Level | Basis |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| >99% sensitive for T21 | 1A | Meta-analysis of >100,000 cases |
| Does not replace diagnostic testing | 1A | ACOG / SMFM |
