## Clinical Definition

Doppler velicometry uses ultrasound to assess blood flow velocity waveforms in fetal and uterine vessels. It is a critical tool for identifying placental insufficiency, fetal hypoxemia, and guiding delivery timing in high-risk pregnancies (1A).

## Key Vessels

**Umbilical Artery (UA):**
- Normal: low impedance, forward end-diastolic flow
- AEDF / REDF: severe placental compromise; urgent delivery (1A)

**Middle Cerebral Artery (MCA):**
- Brain-sparing reflex: ↓ PI in fetal hypoxemia
- MCA-PSV > 1.5 MoM: anemia (parvovirus, alloimmunization)

**Uterine Artery:**
- Notching / high resistance: predicts preeclampsia and FGR

**Ductus Venosus:**
- Absent/reversed a-wave: imminent fetal decompensation (2B)
