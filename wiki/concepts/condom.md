# Condom

The **male condom** is the most commonly used barrier contraceptive method and the only contraceptive that reduces transmission of sexually transmitted infections (STIs).

## Types

| Material | Characteristics |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Latex** | Most common; effective barrier; oil degrades |
| **Polyurethane** | Thinner; heat conductive; oil-compatible |
| **Polyisoprene** | Latex-free stretch; suitable for latex allergy |
| **Lambskin** | Natural membrane; pore size permits viral passage — **not** for STI prevention |

## Effectiveness

- **Perfect use**: 98% (2% failure)
- **Typical use**: 87% (13% failure)
- STI protection: Highly effective against HIV and gonorrhea; moderate for HPV, HSV (skin not covered)

## Proper Use

1. Check expiration date
2. Open carefully (no teeth/nails)
3. Pinch reservoir, roll to base of erect penis
4. Hold rim during withdrawal to prevent spillage
5. Single use only

## Lubrication

**Safe**: Water-based or silicone-based
**Unsafe with latex**: Oil-based products (petroleum jelly, mineral oil, massage oils) — cause degradation

## Advantages

- No hormonal side effects
- STI protection (only method)
- Immediate availability
- No prescription required
- May reduce premature ejaculation

## Disadvantages

- Requires partner cooperation
- Sensory reduction (variable)
- Latex allergy (rare)
- High typical-use failure rate
