## Clinical Definition

Induction of labor is artificial initiation of uterine contractions before spontaneous onset to achieve vaginal delivery. It is one of the most common obstetric interventions (1A).

## Indications

- Postterm pregnancy (≥ 41 weeks)
- Prelabor rupture of membranes at term
- Chorioamnionitis
- Maternal medical conditions (preeclampsia, diabetes, cholestasis)
- Fetal: FGR, Oligohydramnios, isoimmunization, demise
- Elective at 39 weeks (contested — ARRIVE trial showed reduced cesarean in nulliparous) (1B)

## Methods

**Cervical Ripening (unfavorable cervix):**
- Prostaglandins: Dinoprostone (vaginal insert or gel), misoprostol (25 mcg vaginal)
- Mechanical: Foley catheter (30 mL), hygroscopic dilators
- Membrane stripping

**Labor Induction:**
- Oxytocin IV infusion (primary pharmacologic agent)
- Amniotomy after cervical ripening

## Contraindications

- Placenta previa, vasa previa
- Transverse or oblique lie
- Prior classical cesarean or extensive uterine surgery
- Umbilical cord prolapse
- Active genital herpes

## Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence Level | Basis |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Induction at 41 weeks reduces stillbirth | 1A | Cochrane + RCTs |
| ARRIVE: elective 39-week induction reduces cesarean in nulliparous | 1B | ARRIVE RCT |
| Prostaglandins effective for cervical ripening | 1A | Cochrane |
| Foley catheter as effective as prostaglandins | 1B | RCT data |
