## Clinical Definition

Mental health disorders are common in pregnancy and postpartum. Depression affects ~10–20% of pregnant women; anxiety is equally prevalent. Perinatal mental health significantly impacts maternal and child outcomes (2B).

## Screening

- Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS) — validated in pregnancy and postpartum
- PHQ-9, GAD-7
- USPSTF recommends screening for depression in pregnant/postpartum women (1B)

## Treatment

- **Psychotherapy:** CBT, IPT first-line (1B)
- **SSRIs:** Sertraline, fluoxetine generally preferred; avoid paroxetine if possible
- **Benzodiazepines:** Avoid if possible; use lowest dose short-term if needed
- **Lithium:** Use with caution; dose changes in pregnancy; avoid in first trimester if possible

## Risks of Untreated Maternal Depression

- Preterm birth
- Low birth weight
- Poor attachment / bonding
- Long-term child neurodevelopmental effects
- Postpartum depression, suicidal ideation
