## Clinical Definition

Fetal lung maturity refers to the development of surfactant-producing type II pneumocytes sufficient to prevent respiratory distress syndrome (RDS) after birth. Maturity is generally achieved by 34–36 weeks gestation (1A).

## Testing (Historical / Selective Use)

- **Lecithin:Sphingomyelin (L:S) ratio:** > 2.0 mature
- **Phosphatidylglycerol (PG):** Present = mature
- **Surfactant/Albumin ratio (TDx-FLM):** > 55 mg/g mature
- **Lamellar body count:** > 50,000/μL mature

## Clinical Practice

- **Antenatal corticosteroids:** Betamethasone or dexamethasone before 34 weeks reduces RDS, IVH, NEC, mortality (1A)
- Rescue course if < 34 weeks and > 7 days from initial course
- FLM testing rarely used today due to widespread steroid use and gestational-age-based management
