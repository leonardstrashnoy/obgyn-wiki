# HPV Screening

**HPV screening** uses high-risk human papillomavirus testing, alone or combined with cytology, to detect patients at risk for cervical precancer and cancer. Persistent high-risk HPV infection precedes most cervical cancers, so HPV-based screening is the central prevention strategy.

## Screening Modalities

| Modality | Strength | Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| Cytology (Pap) | Specific, historically validated | Less sensitive than HPV testing |
| Primary HPV test | Highest sensitivity for CIN3+ | Requires validated assay and age-appropriate use |
| Cotesting | High sensitivity and reassurance when both negative | More positives and follow-up than primary HPV alone |
| HPV genotyping | Identifies HPV 16/18 high-risk results | Does not replace full risk assessment |

## Age-Based Framework

Local guideline intervals vary; the general evidence-based pattern is:

- Younger than 21: routine screening not recommended in average-risk patients.
- Ages 21-29: cytology-based screening is commonly used; HPV triage may be used for selected abnormalities.
- Ages 30-65: primary HPV testing, cotesting, or cytology at guideline-defined intervals.
- Older than 65: screening can stop after adequate negative prior screening and no high-risk history.

High-risk groups, including immunosuppressed patients or those with prior CIN2+, require individualized surveillance.

## Interpreting Results

Management is risk-based. The same current result can have different actions depending on prior negative HPV tests, prior abnormalities, treatment history, HPV genotype, and age.

Important high-risk patterns:

- HPV 16 or 18 positive
- HSIL cytology
- ASC-H cytology
- AGC or AIS cytology
- Persistent HPV positivity
- Prior CIN2+ treatment

## Follow-up Actions

Possible actions include:

1. Return to routine screening.
2. Repeat HPV-based testing at 1 year or another surveillance interval.
3. Colposcopy with biopsy.
4. Expedited treatment for selected very high-risk combinations.
5. Long-term surveillance after CIN2+ treatment.

## Prevention Linkage

HPV vaccination reduces cervical precancer caused by vaccine-type HPV. Screening is still needed after vaccination because vaccines do not cover all oncogenic HPV types and many patients were exposed before vaccination.

## Evidence Summary

| Claim / Intervention | Evidence Level | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| High-risk HPV testing has higher sensitivity than cytology for CIN3+ | 1A | High |
| Negative HPV-based testing confers low short-term CIN3+ risk | 1A | High |
| Screening before age 21 causes more harm than benefit in average-risk patients | 2A | High |
| Risk-based follow-up reduces unnecessary colposcopy while preserving cancer prevention | 2A | High |
| HPV vaccination complements but does not eliminate screening | 1A | High |
