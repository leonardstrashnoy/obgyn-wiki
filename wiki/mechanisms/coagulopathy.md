## Clinical Definition

Coagulopathy in obstetrics refers to acquired or consumptive disorders of hemostasis leading to impaired clot formation. The most common triggers are massive hemorrhage, placental abruption, amniotic fluid embolism, and sepsis (2B).

## Pathophysiology

**Obstetric DIC triggers:**
- Tissue factor release from placenta/decidua into maternal circulation
- Amniotic fluid procoagulant factors
- Endothelial injury from hemorrhagic shock

**Massive hemorrhage coagulopathy:**
- Consumption of clotting factors and platelets
- Hypothermia, acidosis, dilution ("lethal triad")
- Fibrinolysis activation

## Management

- **Treat underlying cause**
- **Blood product replacement:** FFP, cryoprecipitate/fibrinogen, platelets
- **Tranexamic acid** if fibrinolysis present (1A — WOMAN trial)
- **Factor concentrates:** Fibrinogen concentrate, PCCs
- **Recombinant factor VIIa:** Last resort in refractory hemorrhage
