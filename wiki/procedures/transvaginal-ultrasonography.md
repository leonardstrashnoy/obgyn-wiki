## Clinical Definition

Transvaginal ultrasonography (TVS) is an ultrasound imaging technique using a high-frequency probe placed in the vagina, providing superior resolution of pelvic structures compared to transabdominal imaging. It is the gold standard for early pregnancy evaluation and ectopic pregnancy diagnosis (1A).

## Indications

- Early pregnancy dating and viability
- Ectopic pregnancy diagnosis
- Cervical length measurement
- Ovarian / uterine pathology evaluation
- Follicle monitoring (fertility treatment)
- Early fetal anatomy (11–14 weeks)

## Advantages

- Higher frequency probe = better resolution
- Closer proximity to pelvic organs
- No need for full bladder
- Detects IUP earlier (~4.5–5 weeks vs 6 weeks TAS)
