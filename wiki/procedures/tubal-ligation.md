# Tubal Ligation

**Tubal ligation** is the most common method of permanent female sterilization worldwide. It involves occlusion or interruption of fallopian tubes to prevent sperm-egg interaction.

## Techniques

### Postpartum
- **Pomeroy**: Window in tube; segment excised; ends ligated
- **Parkland**: Similar to Pomeroy with modified technique
- Timing: Within 24 hours postpartum or 6 weeks postpartum

### Interval (Non-puerperal)
- **Laparoscopic**: Clips (Hulka-Clemens, Filshie), bands (Falope ring), electrocautery (bipolar)
- **Minilaparotomy**: Small incision; similar occlusion methods
- **Hysteroscopic**: Essure (discontinued in US 2018)

## Efficacy

- **Failure rate**: 0.5-1% lifetime (varies by method)
- **Ectopic risk**: If failure occurs, 1/3 are ectopic pregnancies

## Reversibility

- **Microsurgical anastomosis**: 40-80% pregnancy rate (varies by age, method, tube remaining length)
- Less effective than IVF for many couples
- Preoperative counseling should emphasize permanence

## Risks

| Complication | Rate |
|--------------|------|
| Surgical complications | <2% |
| Failure | 0.5-1% lifetime |
| Ectopic pregnancy | 1/3 of failures |
| Regret (young age) | Up to 20% |

## Counseling Points

- **Permanent**: No method 100% reversible
- **No hormone changes**: Menstrual pattern unchanged
- **Does not protect against STIs**
- **Consider vasectomy**: Safer, less expensive, more effective

## Alternatives

| Alternative | Reversible | Efficacy |
|-------------|------------|----------|
| **Vasectomy** | Reversal possible | 99.9% |
| **LNG-IUD** | Yes | 99.8% |
| **Implant** | Yes | 99.95% |
