## Clinical Definition

Hysterectomy is the surgical removal of the uterus. In obstetrics, it is a life-saving procedure for uncontrollable postpartum hemorrhage, placenta accreta spectrum, or uterine rupture (1A).

## Obstetric Indications

- Refractory uterine atony not responding to medical/surgical measures
- Placenta percreta with bladder invasion
- Uterine rupture not repairable
- Uterine atony + failed compression sutures / embolization

## Types

- **Subtotal (supracervical):** Faster, fewer urologic complications; cervix retained
- **Total:** Cervix removed; standard for malignancy and most obstetric cases
- **Radical:** Parametrium + vaginal cuff removal; for gynecologic oncology

## Complications

- Hemorrhage, infection, DVT/PE
- Ureteral / bladder injury (1–2%)
- Fistula formation
- Loss of fertility
- Psychological impact
