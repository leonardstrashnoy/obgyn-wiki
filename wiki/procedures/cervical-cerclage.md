## Clinical Definition

Cervical cerclage is a surgical procedure involving placement of a suture around the cervix to prevent premature cervical dilatation and second-trimester pregnancy loss or preterm birth in women with cervical incompetence (1B).

## Indications

- **History-indicated:** Prior second-trimester loss or preterm birth from painless cervical dilatation (2B)
- **Ultrasound-indicated:** TVCL < 25 mm before 24 weeks in singleton with prior spontaneous preterm birth (1B)
- **Rescue/emergency:** Acute painless cervical dilatation with visible membranes in second trimester (2B)

## Technique Key Points

- **McDonald:** Purse-string suture at cervicovaginal junction; most common; removed at 36–37 weeks or at labor onset
- **Shirodkar:** Dissection of bladder reflection, suture placed higher near internal os; for very short cervix or failed McDonald
- **Transabdominal:** Preconception or early pregnancy; reserved after failed vaginal cerclage or congenital absence

## Evidence for Efficacy

- Ultrasound-indicated cerclage in singletons with prior PTB and TVCL < 25 mm reduces preterm delivery before 35 weeks (1B — Cervical Incompetence Prevention Randomized Cerclage Trial)
- History-indicated cerclage: evidence weaker; based on historical cohorts (2B)
- Cerclage is **detrimental** in multiple gestations with short cervix (1B — do not use in twins)

## Contraindications and Precautions

- Active bleeding, infection, ruptured membranes
- Regular contractions / active preterm labor
- Fetal anomalies or demise
- Multiple gestation (not beneficial, may be harmful)
- Risks: PPROM, chorioamnionitis, cervical laceration at delivery, suture displacement

## Complications

- Preterm premature rupture of membranes
- Chorioamnionitis
- Cervical laceration or tear at delivery
- Suture erosion or displacement
- Failed procedure with pregnancy loss
