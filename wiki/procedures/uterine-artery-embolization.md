## Clinical Definition

UAE is a minimally invasive interventional radiology procedure involving selective catheterization and embolization of uterine arteries to control hemorrhage or treat symptomatic fibroids (2B).

## Obstetric Indications

- Refractory postpartum hemorrhage (after failed medical / surgical management)
- Placenta accreta spectrum (planned adjunct or emergent)
- Arteriovenous malformation
- Cervical / vaginal laceration with arterial bleeding

## Technique

- Femoral artery access → bilateral uterine artery catheterization
- Embolization with polyvinyl alcohol particles or gelatin sponge
- Preserves uterus and fertility in most cases

## Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence Level | Basis |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Effective for refractory PPH | 2B | Case series + cohorts |
| Preserves fertility in ~80% | 2B | Cohort follow-up |
| Complication rate ~5–10% | 2B | Cohort data |
| Faster recovery than hysterectomy | 2B | Cohort comparison |
