## Clinical Definition

Laparoscopy (minimally invasive surgery) is the preferred surgical approach for many gynecologic and obstetric procedures, including diagnosis and treatment of ectopic pregnancy, ovarian pathology, and sterilization (1B).

## Indications in OB/GYN

- Ectopic pregnancy (salpingostomy or salpingectomy) (1B)
- Ovarian torsion / cystectomy
- Tubal sterilization
- Diagnostic evaluation of pelvic pain / infertility
- Lymph node evaluation in gynecologic oncology

## Technique Key Points

- Pneumoperitoneum with CO₂ insufflation (safe in pregnancy up to 16–20 weeks; beyond this, open entry or left upper quadrant entry may be preferred)
- Trendelenburg positioning (caution in late pregnancy)
- Energy sources: bipolar, monopolar, harmonic scalpel

## Evidence for Efficacy

- Laparoscopic salpingostomy for ectopic pregnancy: faster recovery, less blood loss, comparable fertility to laparotomy (1B)
- Preferred over laparotomy for hemodynamically stable patients with ectopic pregnancy (1B)
- Safe in pregnancy when performed by experienced surgeons with appropriate modifications (2B)

## Contraindications and Precautions

- Hemodynamic instability / severe hemorrhage (laparotomy preferred)
- Massive hemoperitoneum
- Severe cardiopulmonary disease (CO₂ pneumoperitoneum concerns)
- Late pregnancy (relative — increasing difficulty with uterine size)

## Adverse Effects

- Vascular or bowel injury (1–3 per 1000)
- CO₂ embolism (rare)
- Postoperative shoulder pain (referred diaphragmatic irritation)
- Subcutaneous emphysema
- Port-site metastasis (oncology concern)
