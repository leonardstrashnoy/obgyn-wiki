## Clinical Definition

Group B Streptococcus (Streptococcus agalactiae) is a gram-positive bacterium colonizing the genitourinary and GI tracts. Maternal colonization is the primary risk factor for early-onset neonatal sepsis (EONS) (1A).

## Screening

- Universal vaginal-rectal culture at 35–37 weeks
- NAAT for intrapartum testing if unknown status

## Prevention

- **Intrapartum antibiotic prophylaxis (IAP):** Penicillin G 5 million units IV, then 2.5 million units q4h until delivery
- **Alternative:** Ampicillin; cefazolin if penicillin allergy (non-anaphylactic); vancomycin if high-risk anaphylaxis
- IAP reduces GBS EONS by ~80% (1A)
