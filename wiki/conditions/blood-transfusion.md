## Clinical Definition

Blood transfusion in obstetrics is required when hemorrhage leads to significant blood loss and anemia. Massive transfusion protocols (MTP) activate when blood loss > 1500 mL or hemodynamic instability (1A).

## Triggers for Transfusion

- Hemoglobin < 7 g/dL (or < 8 g/dL with ongoing hemorrhage)
- Hemodynamic instability despite crystalloid
- DIC with active bleeding

## Components

- **RBC:** Restore oxygen-carrying capacity
- **FFP:** Replace clotting factors (PT/aPTT > 1.5× normal)
- **Cryoprecipitate / fibrinogen concentrate:** Fibrinogen < 100–150 mg/dL
- **Platelets:** < 50,000 with active bleeding or < 10–20,000
- **Tranexamic acid:** 1 g IV in PPH (WOMAN trial) (1A)

## Massive Transfusion Protocol

- 1:1:1 ratio RBC:FFP:Platelets (extrapolated from trauma literature; applied in obstetric hemorrhage)
- Early TXA
- Fibrinogen concentrate or cryoprecipitate early
- Cell salvage acceptable in most obstetric settings (contaminated by amniotic fluid but safe with leukodepletion filtration)

## Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence Level | Basis |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| MTP improves survival in massive hemorrhage | 1A | Trauma data + obstetric extrapolation |
| TXA reduces mortality in PPH | 1A | WOMAN trial |
| Fibrinogen < 100–150 mg/dL requires replacement | 2B | Trauma + obstetric data |
| Cell salvage safe with filtration | 2B | Cohort studies |
