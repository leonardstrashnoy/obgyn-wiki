## Clinical Definition

Vaginal bleeding in pregnancy is a common symptom with etiologies ranging from physiologic (implantation) to life-threatening (abruption, previa). The timing, amount, and associated symptoms guide evaluation (1B).

## First Trimester

- **Physiologic:** Implantation bleeding (light, transient)
- **Threatened abortion:** Bleeding with closed cervix, viable pregnancy
- **Inevitable/incomplete abortion:** Bleeding with open cervix
- **Ectopic pregnancy:** Bleeding + pain + adnexal mass
- **Molar pregnancy:** Bleeding + hyperemesis + very high hCG
- **Cervical lesions:** Ectropion, polyp, carcinoma

## Second Trimester

- **Cervical insufficiency:** Painless bleeding + dilatation
- **Placenta previa:** Painless bleeding (recurrent, bright red)
- **Abruption:** Painful bleeding + uterine tenderness
- **Vasa previa:** Painless bleeding + fetal bradycardia (fetal vessel rupture)

## Third Trimester

- **Placenta previa:** Painless, recurrent
- **Placental abruption:** Painful, uterine tenderness, fetal distress
- **Bloody show:** Mucus plug loss before labor (normal)
- **Vasa previa:** Fetal vessel rupture → emergency

## Management Principles

- **Assess hemodynamic status:** Vitals, orthostatics, blood count
- **Determine source:** Speculum exam (carefully if previa not excluded), ultrasound for placental location/fetal status
- **No digital exams** if undiagnosed bleeding in second/third trimester until previa excluded
- **Type and screen / crossmatch** if significant bleeding
- **Rhogam** if Rh-negative and bleeding in first/second trimester
