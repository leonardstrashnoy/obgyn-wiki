# Abnormal Uterine Bleeding

**Acute abnormal uterine bleeding (AUB)** is an important cause of gynecologic morbidity requiring distinct therapeutic approaches from obstetric hemorrhage. Management relies on antifibrinolytic and hormonal therapy, with protocol-driven care showing increasing evidence of benefit over traditional stepwise escalation.

## Scope

Abnormal uterine bleeding encompasses menstrual irregularities including heavy menstrual bleeding, intermenstrual bleeding, postmenstrual bleeding, and acute uterine hemorrhage. This page focuses on acute gynecologic AUB management in non-pregnant patients.

## Etiology Classification (PALM-COEIN)

Structural causes (PALM):
- **P**olyps
- **A**denomyosis
- **L**eiomyomas (fibroids)
- **M**alignancy/hyperplasia

Non-structural causes (COEIN):
- **C**oagulopathy
- **O**vulatory dysfunction
- **E**ndometrial causes
- **I**atrogenic
- **N**ot yet classified

## Diagnosis

### Clinical Assessment
- Quantification of bleeding (pads/tampons, pictorial blood loss assessment chart)
- Vital signs for hemodynamic stability
- Laboratory: CBC, coagulation studies (if indicated), hCG (to exclude pregnancy)
- Imaging: Transvaginal ultrasound first-line for structural causes
- Endometrial biopsy: Indicated for age ≥45 years or risk factors for endometrial cancer

## Management

### Acute Hemorrhage

**First-line therapy** ^[raw/articles/management-of-obstetric-and-gynecologic-hemorrhage-42112220.md]

1. **Antifibrinolytic therapy** — Tranexamic acid
   - Evidence: Early administration effective for acute AUB
   - Level: 1A (systematic review of RCTs)

2. **Hormonal therapy**
   - Acute: High-dose estrogen or combined oral contraceptives
   - Maintenance: Cyclic progestins, LNG-IUD, or combined hormonal contraception

3. **Supportive care**
   - Hemodynamic monitoring
   - Blood transfusion per restrictive protocols for stable patients ^[raw/articles/management-of-obstetric-and-gynecologic-hemorrhage-42112220.md]

### Indications for Surgical Intervention

- Failed medical management
- Hemodynamic instability
- Suspected malignancy
- Patient preference after counseling

Options: Endometrial ablation hysteroscopic polypectomy/myomectomy, dilation and curettage, or hysterectomy (definitive).
