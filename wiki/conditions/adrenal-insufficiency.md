## Clinical Definition

Adrenal insufficiency is inadequate cortisol production. Primary (Addison disease) is adrenal destruction; secondary is pituitary ACTH deficiency. In obstetrics, Sheehan syndrome is a cause of secondary adrenal insufficiency (2B).

## Obstetric Relevance

- **Sheehan syndrome:** Postpartum pituitary necrosis → ACTH deficiency → adrenal insufficiency
- **Chronic adrenal insufficiency:** Requires stress-dose steroids in labor (hydrocortisone 100 mg IV q8h)
- **Acute adrenal crisis:** Hypotension, hypoglycemia, hyponatremia, hyperkalemia — treat with hydrocortisone + fluids emergently
