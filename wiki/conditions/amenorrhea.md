# Amenorrhea

**Amenorrhea** is the absence of menstrual bleeding. It is classified as primary (never had menses by age 15) or secondary (absence of menses for >3 cycles or 6 months in a previously menstruating woman).

## Definitions

| Type | Definition | Prevalence |
|------|------------|------------|
| **Primary** | No menarche by age 15 (>2.5 SD above mean age) | 0.3% |
| **Secondary** | No menses for >3 cycles or 6 months (previously regular) | 3-5% |

*Note: Pregnancy is the most common cause of secondary amenorrhea and must always be excluded first.*

## Etiology

### Primary Amenorrhea

**Anatomic/outflow tract (15%)**
- Müllerian agenesis (MRKH syndrome)
- Imperforate hymen
- Transverse vaginal septum
- Complete androgen insensitivity

**Ovarian (40%)**
- Gonadal dysgenesis (Turner syndrome)
- Pure gonadal dysgenesis (46,XX or 46,XY)
- Premature ovarian insufficiency (rare in adolescents)
- Enzyme defects (17α-hydroxylase, aromatase)

**Hypothalamic/pituitary (40%)**
- Constitutional delay of puberty
- Functional hypothalamic amenorrhea (stress, weight loss, exercise)
- Kallmann syndrome ( anosmia + HH)
- Craniopharyngioma
- Pituitary adenoma

**Other (5%)**
- Thyroid disease
- Hyperprolactinemia
- Chronic illness

### Secondary Amenorrhea

**Physiologic**
- Pregnancy (most common)
- Lactation
- Menopause

**Hypothalamic (35%)**
- Functional hypothalamic amenorrhea (FHA): weight loss, exercise, stress, eating disorders
- Chronic illness

**Pituitary (15%)**
- Hyperprolactinemia
- Hypopituitarism (Sheehan syndrome, apoplexy)
- Empty sella syndrome

**Ovarian (45%)**
- Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) — most common endocrine cause
- Premature ovarian insufficiency (<40 years)
- Oophorectomy
- Chemotherapy/radiation

**Outflow tract (<5%)**
- Asherman syndrome (intrauterine adhesions)
- Cervical stenosis

## Evaluation

### Initial Workup (All Patients)

1. **Pregnancy test** (hCG) — always first
2. **History**: Menstrual pattern, galactorrhea, hot flashes, hirsutism, weight change, exercise, stress, medications
3. **Physical exam**: Signs of hyperandrogenism, thyroid, BMI, breast development, pelvic exam or MRI for anatomic causes

### Laboratory Tests

| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **FSH, LH** | Distinguish ovarian vs hypothalamic/pituitary; high FSH = ovarian failure |
| **Estradiol** | Confirms hypoestrogenism |
| **TSH** | Exclude thyroid disease |
| **Prolactin** | Exclude prolactinoma |
| **Testosterone/DHEA-S** | Evaluate PCOS or virilizing tumor |
| **Karyotype** | If primary amenorrhea with high FSH (Turner, AIS) |

### Imaging

- **Pelvic ultrasound**: Ovarian morphology (PCOS), anatomic anomalies, endometrial thickness
- **MRI brain/pituitary**: If prolactin elevated or suspected pituitary lesion
- **Hysterosalpingography or hysteroscopy**: Suspected Asherman syndrome

### Diagnostic Algorithm

```
Secondary amenorrhea → Pregnancy test
  ↓ negative
FSH/LH/estradiol
  ↓
Low/normal FSH → Hypothalamic/pituitary → Further testing (prolactin, imaging)
High FSH → Ovarian failure → Karyotype if <30 years
Normal FSH/high testosterone → PCOS
```

## Management

### Hypothalamic

**Functional hypothalamic amenorrhea** (Level: 2A)
- Lifestyle modification: weight gain, reduce exercise intensity, stress management
- Cognitive behavioral therapy
- Goal: Resume menses to protect bone density
- If prolonged: Cyclic estrogen/progestin for bone protection

### Pituitary

**Hyperprolactinemia** (Level: 1A)
- Dopamine agonists (cabergoline first-line; bromocriptine alternative)
- Surgery if macroadenoma with mass effect
- Monitor prolactin; menses usually resume after normalization

**Sheehan syndrome**: Hormone replacement as indicated

### Ovarian

**PCOS** (Level: 2A)
- See [[polycystic-ovary-syndrome]]
- Lifestyle modification, hormonal contraception, metformin

**Premature ovarian insufficiency** (Level: 2A)
- Hormone replacement therapy until natural menopause age (~50 years)
- Fertility: Egg donation (only reliable option)
- Bone protection, cardiovascular monitoring
- Genetic counseling if karyotype abnormal

### Outflow Tract

**Asherman syndrome** (Level: 2B)
- Hysteroscopic adhesiolysis
- Postoperative estrogen therapy to promote endometrial regrowth
- Intrauterine balloon or IUD to prevent recurrence

**Cervical stenosis**: Dilation

### Primary Amenorrhea Specific

**Turner syndrome**: Growth hormone during childhood, then estrogen replacement at pubertal age
**Müllerian agenesis**: Psychosocial support, vaginal dilation or surgery for sexual function; surrogacy for reproduction

## Complications

| Complication | Risk Population | Prevention |
|--------------|-----------------|------------|
| **Osteoporosis** | Hypoestrogenism (FHA, POI, athletes) | Weight restoration, estrogen therapy |
| **Cardiovascular disease** | POI | Estrogen replacement |
| **Endometrial hyperplasia/cancer** | PCOS (unopposed estrogen) | Cyclic progestin or IUD |
| **Infertility** | All causes | Fertility preservation counseling |
