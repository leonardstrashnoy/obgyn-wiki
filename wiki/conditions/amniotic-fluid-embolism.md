## Clinical Definition

Amniotic fluid embolism (AFE) is a rare, catastrophic obstetric emergency characterized by sudden onset of hypoxia, hypotension, and coagulopathy during labor, delivery, or immediately postpartum. Likely an anaphylactoid reaction to fetal antigens rather than true embolism (4).

## Epidemiology

- Incidence: 1.9–6.1 per 100,000 deliveries
- Mortality: 20–60% (leading cause of maternal death in developed countries)
- Fetal mortality: ~20–40%

## Clinical Presentation

**Phase 1:**
- Sudden dyspnea, hypoxia, cyanosis
- Severe hypotension / cardiovascular collapse
- Altered mental status, seizures

**Phase 2:**
- Disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC)
- Severe hemorrhage (often uterine atony unresponsive)

## Management

- **Immediate resuscitation:** Intubation, ventilation, circulatory support
- **Blood products:** Massive transfusion protocol for DIC
- **Uterotonics** for atony
- **Hysterectomy** if refractory bleeding (life-saving)
- ICU management; no specific antidote
