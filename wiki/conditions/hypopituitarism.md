## Clinical Definition

Hypopituitarism is deficiency of one or more pituitary hormones. Postpartum hypopituitarism is most commonly due to Sheehan syndrome (ischemic necrosis from hemorrhagic shock) (2B).

## Hormone Deficiencies

- **GH:** Fatigue, poor lactation (if prolactin also affected)
- **ACTH:** Adrenal insufficiency, hypotension, hypoglycemia
- **TSH:** Hypothyroidism
- **LH/FSH:** Amenorrhea, infertility, loss of libido
- **Prolactin:** Failure to lactate
- **ADH:** Diabetes insipidus (rare in Sheehan)

## Management

- Lifelong hormone replacement (thyroxine, hydrocortisone, estrogen/progesterone, GH as indicated)
- Hydrocortisone must precede thyroxine (thyroxine increases cortisol metabolism, can precipitate crisis)
