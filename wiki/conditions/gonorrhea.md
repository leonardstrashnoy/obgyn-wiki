## Clinical Definition

Neisseria gonorrhoeae is a gram-negative diplococcus causing urethritis, cervicitis, and pelvic inflammatory disease. Increasing antimicrobial resistance is a major global health threat (1A).

## Diagnosis

- NAAT (urine, vaginal, cervical, pharyngeal, rectal)
- Culture with antimicrobial susceptibility testing recommended if treatment failure suspected

## Treatment

- **Uncomplicated:** Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM (1 g if ≥150 kg) + doxycycline 100 mg BID × 7 days if chlamydia not excluded (CDC 2021) (1A)
- **Alternative:** Cefixime 800 mg PO if ceftriaxone unavailable (1B)
- No fluoroquinolones or oral azithromycin monotherapy due to resistance

## Complications

- PID, tubo-ovarian abscess
- Disseminated gonococcal infection (DGI): arthritis, dermatitis, tenosynovitis
- Infertility, ectopic pregnancy
- Neonatal ophthalmia neonatorum (prevented by erythromycin ointment)

## Resistance

- Extended-spectrum cephalosporin resistance emerging (Japan, Europe, US)
- Surveillance and culture with AST critical
- Dual therapy no longer recommended if chlamydia excluded (ceftriaxone monotherapy sufficient)
