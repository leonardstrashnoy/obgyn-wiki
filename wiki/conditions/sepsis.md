## Clinical Definition

Sepsis is life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. Septic shock is sepsis with circulatory failure requiring vasopressors (1A — Sepsis-3 definition).

## Obstetric Sepsis

- **Puerperal sepsis:** Postpartum uterine infection (historically leading cause of maternal death)
- **Chorioamnionitis:** Intra-amniotic infection
- **Tubo-ovarian abscess:** Pelvic infection
- **Pyelonephritis:** UTI complication in pregnancy
- **Necrotizing fasciitis:** Rare, catastrophic (cesarean wound, episiotomy)

## Management

- **Hour-1 bundle:** Blood cultures, broad-spectrum antibiotics, lactate, fluids, vasopressors if MAP < 65
- **Antibiotics within 1 hour:** Meropenem, vancomycin, clindamycin (broad coverage)
- **Source control:** Drainage of abscess, debridement of necrotic tissue, delivery if chorioamnionitis
- **Obstetric modifications:** Physiologic leukocytosis in pregnancy; higher baseline lactate; lower MAP targets may be appropriate
