## Clinical Definition

Maternal mortality is the death of a woman during pregnancy or within 42 days of termination of pregnancy, from any cause related to or aggravated by the pregnancy or its management (WHO definition) (2B).

## Leading Causes (US / Developed)

1. Cardiovascular disease
2. Non-obstetric trauma / homicide / suicide (increasingly recognized)
3. Hemorrhage (postpartum)
4. Infection / sepsis
5. Amniotic fluid embolism
6. Preeclampsia / eclampsia
7. Thromboembolism

## Disparities

- Black women: 3–4× higher maternal mortality than white women in US (2B)
- Native American / Alaska Native: 2–3× higher
- Most deaths preventable with timely, quality care

## Prevention Strategies

- Hemorrhage protocols (quantitative blood loss, TXA, uterotonics)
- Maternal early warning systems (MEWS)
- Cardiovascular disease screening
- Postpartum care expansion ("fourth trimester")
- Address structural racism and care access
