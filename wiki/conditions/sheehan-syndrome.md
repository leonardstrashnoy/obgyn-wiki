## Clinical Definition

Sheehan syndrome is postpartum hypopituitarism caused by ischemic necrosis of the pituitary due to severe hypotension and hypovolemia from obstetric hemorrhage. The enlarged pituitary in pregnancy is particularly susceptible to vascular insufficiency (2B).

## Presentation

- **Acute:** Failure to lactate postpartum (deficient prolactin), hypotension, hypoglycemia
- **Chronic:** Progressive panhypopituitarism — amenorrhea, hypothyroidism, adrenal insufficiency, loss of axillary/pubic hair

## Diagnosis

- **Hormone testing:** Low prolactin, low cortisol, low TSH/T4, low LH/FSH/estradiol, low GH/IGF-1
- **MRI:** Empty sella or partially empty pituitary

## Management

- **Lifelong hormone replacement:** Levothyroxine, hydrocortisone, estrogen/progesterone, GH as indicated
- **Acute adrenal crisis:** Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV; fluid resuscitation
