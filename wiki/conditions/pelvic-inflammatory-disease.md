## Clinical Definition

PID is an infection-induced inflammation of the upper female genital tract (endometrium, fallopian tubes, ovaries, peritoneum), typically ascending from lower tract infections. It is a leading preventable cause of ectopic pregnancy and infertility (2B).

## Epidemiology

- Estimated 4.4% of US women report lifetime PID diagnosis
- Highest incidence in ages 15–25
- Risk of ectopic pregnancy increases 6- to 10-fold after PID
- Risk of infertility: 8% after 1 episode, 20% after 2, 40% after 3

## Etiology

- **Neisseria gonorrhoeae** and **Chlamydia trachomatis** most common
- Polymicrobial (anaerobes, Gardnerella, H. influenzae, etc.)
- Postpartum / postabortal infection

## Diagnosis

Clinical diagnosis (no single gold standard):
- Lower abdominal pain + adnexal tenderness or cervical motion tenderness
- Fever, abnormal discharge, elevated ESR/CRP
- NAAT for gonorrhea/chlamydia
- Ultrasound: hydrosalpinx, tubo-ovarian abscess

## Management

- **Outpatient:** Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM + doxycycline 100 mg PO BID × 14 days + metronidazole 500 mg PO BID × 14 days (CDC 2021)
- **Inpatient:** Severe illness, pregnancy, TOA, failed outpatient therapy
- **Surgery:** Drainage of TOA if rupture risk or failed antibiotics
