## Clinical Definition

Placenta previa is placental implantation over or adjacent to the internal cervical os. Complete previa covers the os; marginal/partial approaches or touches it; low-lying is within 2 cm (1B).

## Epidemiology

- Incidence: ~0.5% at term (many "resolve" as lower segment develops)
- Risk increases with prior cesarean, uterine surgery, advanced age, smoking, multiple gestation

## Presentation

- **Painless vaginal bleeding** in second or third trimester (hallmark)
- Bleeding often recurrent and unpredictable
- May present with preterm labor or emergency hemorrhage

## Management

- **No vaginal exams** (can trigger massive hemorrhage)
- **Cesarean delivery:** Mandatory for complete previa
- **Timing:** 36–37 weeks for stable complete previa; earlier if hemorrhage
- **Hemorrhage:** Resuscitation, blood products, emergency cesarean

## Complications

- Massive hemorrhage
- Placenta accreta spectrum (especially with prior cesarean)
- Preterm birth
- Fetal growth restriction
- Vasa previa (if velamentous insertion)
