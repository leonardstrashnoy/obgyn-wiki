## Clinical Definition

Tubo-ovarian abscess (TOA) is a complication of pelvic inflammatory disease where infection localizes to form an abscess involving the fallopian tube and ovary. It is a surgical emergency if ruptured (2B).

## Diagnosis

- Pelvic pain + fever + adnexal mass
- Ultrasound / CT: Complex adnexal mass with septations, debris
- Elevated WBC, CRP
- Culture: Polymicrobial (N. gonorrhoeae, C. trachomatis, anaerobes)

## Management

- **Antibiotics:** Broad-spectrum IV (ceftriaxone + doxycycline + metronidazole)
- **Surgery:** Drainage or adnexectomy if rupture risk, failed antibiotics, or abscess > 8 cm
- **Image-guided drainage:** Interventional radiology drainage for unruptured TOA
