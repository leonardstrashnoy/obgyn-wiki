# Contraception

**Contraception** encompasses methods and devices used to prevent pregnancy. Modern options include hormonal, intrauterine, barrier, permanent, and fertility awareness-based methods.

## Effectiveness

| Tier | Methods | Typical Use Failure Rate |
|------|---------|-------------------------|
| **Most effective** | Sterilization, IUDs, implants | <1% |
| **Effective** | Injectable, pills, patch, ring | 6-9% |
| **Less effective** | Barrier methods | 13-21% |
| **Least effective** | Fertility awareness, withdrawal | 24% |

*Typical use accounts for real-world adherence; perfect use rates are significantly lower.*

## Hormonal Methods

### Combined Estrogen-Progestin

**Combined oral contraceptives (COCs)** (Level: 1A)
- **Efficacy**: 91% typical use, >99% perfect use
- **Mechanism**: Ovulation suppression, cervical mucus thickening, endometrial suppression
- **Benefits**: Cycle control, reduced dysmenorrhea, acne improvement, reduced ovarian/endometrial cancer risk
- **Risks**: VTE (3-9/10,000/year), stroke/MI (rare, age-dependent), BP elevation
- **Contraindications**: Age >35 + smoking, VTE history, migraine with aura, breast cancer, severe cirrhosis, uncontrolled HTN

**Transdermal patch** (Level: 1A)
- Weekly application; same hormones as COCs
- Higher estrogen exposure (60% higher) → slightly higher VTE risk

**Vaginal ring** (Level: 1A)
- Monthly use; lower systemic estrogen than patch
- Efficacy comparable to COCs

### Progestin-Only

**Progestin-only pill (POP)** (Level: 1A)
- Minipill: daily, same time (±3 hour window)
- **Mechanism**: Primarily cervical mucus thickening; ovulation suppression variable
- **Advantages**: Safe in lactation, no estrogen risks
- **Disadvantages**: Strict timing adherence, irregular bleeding

**Injectable: Depot medroxyprogesterone acetate (DMPA)** (Level: 1A)
- Dose: 150 mg IM every 3 months
- **Efficacy**: 94% typical use
- **Advantages**: Discreet, reversible, reduces sickle cell crises, endometrial protection
- **Disadvantages**: Delayed return to fertility (median 10 months), bone density loss (recoverable), weight gain
- **Black box**: Decreased bone mineral density (not permanent)

**Implant: Etonogestrel** (Level: 1A)
- Single-rod, subdermal, 3-year duration
- **Efficacy**: >99% (most effective reversible method)
- **Advantages**: Long-acting, rapidly reversible, low maintenance
- **Disadvantages**: Irregular bleeding (most common reason for discontinuation)

## Intrauterine Devices (IUDs)

### Copper IUD
- **Mechanism**: Spermicidal (copper ions), inflammatory response
- **Duration**: 10-12 years
- **Efficacy**: >99%
- **Advantages**: Hormone-free, emergency contraception if inserted ≤5 days post-intercourse
- **Disadvantages**: Heavier, longer, more painful menses; risk of perforation (1/1000 insertion)

### Levonorgestrel IUD (LNG-IUD)
- **Doses**: 52 mg (Mirena/Liletta, 8 years), 19.5 mg (Kyleena, 5 years), 13.5 mg (Skyla, 3 years)
- **Mechanism**: Local progestin → thick cervical mucus, thin endometrium, reduced ovulation (variable)
- **Efficacy**: >99%
- **Advantages**: Reduced bleeding (20% amenorrhea at 1 year), reduced dysmenorrhea, endometrial protection
- **Disadvantages**: Irregular bleeding in first 3-6 months, expulsion (2-5%)

**Insertion precautions**: Pregnancy/STI exclusion, uterine cavity size (sound >6 cm), contraindicated in active pelvic infection, unexplained bleeding

## Barrier Methods

**Male condom** (Level: 1A)
- Only method that reduces STI transmission
- Efficacy: 87% typical use
- Use with water or silicone lubricant (oil degrades latex)

**Female condom**
- Efficacy: 79% typical use
- Protects vulva (more STI coverage)

**Diaphragm/cervical cap with spermicide**
- Efficacy: 88% typical use
- Requires fitting, prescription

## Permanent Methods

**Tubal ligation** (Level: 1A)
- Surgical or laparoscopic occlusion
- **Efficacy**: 99.5%
- **Considerations**: Should be considered permanent; reversal expensive and low success
- **Risk**: Very small ectopic pregnancy risk if failure occurs

**Vasectomy** (male)
- **Efficacy**: 99.9%
- Simpler, safer, less expensive than tubal ligation
- Requires follow-up semen analysis (azoospermia confirmation)

## Emergency Contraception

**Levonorgestrel 1.5 mg** (Plan B) (Level: 1A)
- **Window**: Within 72 hours (effective up to 120 hours with decreasing efficacy)
- **Mechanism**: Delayed ovulation
- **Efficacy**: Reduces pregnancy risk 75-89%
- **Weight consideration**: Reduced efficacy >75 kg; consider copper IUD or ulipristal

**Ulipristal acetate 30 mg** (Ella) (Level: 1A)
- **Window**: Within 120 hours
- **Mechanism**: Progesterone receptor modulator; delays ovulation more effectively near LH surge
- **Advantage**: More effective than LNG in late follicular phase
- **Interaction**: COC initiation should wait 5 days

**Copper IUD** (Level: 1A)
- **Window**: Within 5 days of intercourse
- **Efficacy**: >99% (most effective EC method)
- **Advantage**: Provides ongoing contraception

## Contraception Selection

### By Life Stage

| Stage | Recommended | Caution/Avoid |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Adolescent** | IUDs, implant, COCs | Barrier only (high failure) |
| **Postpartum** | IUD, implant, progestin-only (lactation), DMPA | COCs (if breastfeeding) |
| **Perimenopause** | LNG-IUD, implant, COCs (if healthy) | None specific |
| **>35 + smoker** | Progestin-only, IUD, barrier | COCs |

### Medical Conditions

**Hypertension**
- Avoid: COCs if BP >160/100 or vascular disease
- Safe: Progestin-only, IUDs, barrier

**Migraine**
- Avoid: COCs if migraine with aura (stroke risk)
- Safe: Progestin-only, IUDs

**Diabetes**
- Avoid: COCs if vascular complications
- Safe: Progestin-only, IUDs

**History of breast cancer**
- Generally avoid hormonal methods (consult oncology)
- Safe: Copper IUD, barrier

**Antiepileptic use (enzyme inducers)**
- Avoid: COCs, POP (reduced efficacy)
- Safe: DMPA, IUDs, implant
