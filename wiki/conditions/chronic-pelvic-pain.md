## Clinical Definition

Chronic pelvic pain (CPP) is noncyclic pain lasting >6 months that localizes to the pelvis, anterior abdominal wall at or below the umbilicus, lumbosacral back, or buttocks. It is a symptom with multiple possible etiologies (2B).

## Etiologies

- **Gynecologic:** Endometriosis, adenomyosis, pelvic adhesions, ovarian remnant, pelvic congestion
- **Urologic:** Interstitial cystitis / bladder pain syndrome
- **GI:** IBS, inflammatory bowel disease
- **Musculoskeletal:** Pelvic floor dysfunction, myofascial pain
- **Neuropathic:** Pudendal neuralgia, abdominal cutaneous nerve entrapment
- **Psychological:** Depression, somatization, abuse history

## Evaluation

- Detailed history, pain diary
- Physical exam: abdominal, pelvic, musculoskeletal
- Imaging: TVUS, MRI for deep infiltrating endometriosis
- Cystoscopy, colonoscopy as indicated

## Management

- **Multidisciplinary:** Gynecology, urology, GI, pain medicine, physical therapy, psychology
- **Medical:** NSAIDs, hormonal suppression (OCPs, GnRH agonists, progestins), neuropathic agents (gabapentin, amitriptyline)
- **Surgical:** Laparoscopy with excision/ablation for endometriosis, lysis of adhesions, hysterectomy (last resort)
- **Physical therapy:** Pelvic floor muscle rehabilitation
