## Clinical Definition

Gestational trophoblastic disease (GTD) encompasses a spectrum of placental trophoblastic proliferative disorders including complete and partial hydatidiform moles, invasive mole, choriocarcinoma, and placental site trophoblastic tumor. Choriocarcinoma is malignant and metastasizes (2B).

## Classification

- **Complete mole:** 46,XX (androgenetic); no fetal tissue; high malignant potential (~15–20%)
- **Partial mole:** Triploid (69,XXY or 69,XXX); fetal tissue present; low malignant potential (~1–5%)
- **Invasive mole:** Penetrates myometrium
- **Choriocarcinoma:** Malignant; hematogenous spread to lung, brain, liver
- **PSTT / ETT:** Rare, chemo-resistant, hysterectomy often required

## Diagnosis

- Ultrasound: "snowstorm" appearance (complete mole); focal cystic changes (partial)
- β-hCG: Very high, often > 100,000 IU/L
- Histopathology after evacuation

## Management

- **Evacuation:** Suction D&C for mole; hysterectomy if no future fertility desired
- **Follow-up:** Weekly β-hCG until normal × 3, then monthly × 6 months
- **Chemotherapy:** For persistent GTN (methotrexate or actinomycin-D first-line; EMA-CO for high-risk)
