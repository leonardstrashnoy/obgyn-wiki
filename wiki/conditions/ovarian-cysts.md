# Ovarian Cysts

**Ovarian cysts** are fluid-filled or complex adnexal lesions arising from physiologic ovarian function or benign/malignant pathology. Most simple cysts in reproductive-aged patients are functional and resolve spontaneously; management depends on menopausal status, pregnancy status, symptoms, size, ultrasound morphology, and malignancy risk.

## Scope

This page covers common benign ovarian cysts, initial evaluation of adnexal masses, follow-up thresholds, and urgent complications. It is not a full ovarian cancer staging page; suspicious or malignant features should route to gynecologic oncology evaluation.

## Classification

| Category | Examples | Typical Features |
|---|---|---|
| Functional | Follicular cyst, corpus luteum cyst | Simple or hemorrhagic, reproductive age, often resolves |
| Benign neoplastic | Serous cystadenoma, mucinous cystadenoma, mature teratoma | Persistent, may enlarge, usually benign morphology |
| Endometriotic | Endometrioma | Homogeneous low-level echoes, dysmenorrhea/endometriosis symptoms |
| Inflammatory | Tubo-ovarian abscess | Pain, fever, leukocytosis, PID features |
| Malignant / borderline | Epithelial ovarian cancer, borderline tumor | Solid components, papillary projections, ascites, high-risk Doppler |

## Epidemiology

- Simple ovarian cysts are common incidental findings in premenopausal patients.
- Functional cysts usually resolve within 1-3 menstrual cycles.
- Postmenopausal cysts require more cautious risk stratification because baseline malignancy risk is higher.
- In pregnancy, corpus luteum cysts are common early and usually regress by the second trimester.

## Clinical Presentation

### Asymptomatic

Many cysts are found incidentally on pelvic ultrasound performed for bleeding, pain, infertility, or pregnancy dating.

### Symptomatic

Symptoms may include:

- Unilateral pelvic pain or pressure
- Bloating or early satiety if large
- Dyspareunia
- Acute severe pain with rupture, hemorrhage, or torsion
- Menstrual irregularity with functional cysts

## Diagnosis

### Initial Evaluation

1. **Pregnancy test** in reproductive-aged patients.
2. Assess hemodynamic stability and acute abdomen signs.
3. Characterize pain onset: sudden severe pain suggests torsion, rupture, or hemorrhage.
4. Review menopausal status, family history, prior malignancy, and endometriosis history.

### Ultrasound Characterization

Transvaginal ultrasound is first-line.

Benign features:

- Unilocular simple cyst
- Thin wall
- Anechoic fluid
- No solid components
- No papillary projections
- No ascites

Concerning features:

- Solid areas or mural nodules
- Papillary projections
- Thick septations
- Bilaterality with complex morphology
- Ascites or peritoneal nodularity
- Marked internal vascularity

### Tumor Markers

CA-125 has limited specificity in premenopausal patients because it can be elevated with endometriosis, fibroids, PID, pregnancy, and menstruation. It is more useful in postmenopausal risk stratification or when ultrasound is suspicious.

## Management

### Expectant Management

Appropriate for many asymptomatic simple cysts, especially in premenopausal patients.

General principles:

- Small simple cysts: reassurance and no intervention or short-interval follow-up depending on size and local protocol.
- Functional or hemorrhagic cysts: repeat ultrasound in 6-12 weeks if persistent symptoms, large size, or diagnostic uncertainty.
- Stable classic endometrioma or dermoid: surveillance may be reasonable if asymptomatic and low risk, with counseling about torsion/growth.

### Medical Management

Combined hormonal contraception prevents formation of new functional cysts by suppressing ovulation but does **not** reliably accelerate resolution of an existing functional cyst. Analgesia and observation are appropriate for uncomplicated cysts.

### Surgical Management

Indications include:

- Suspected torsion
- Hemodynamic instability or significant hemoperitoneum from rupture
- Persistent or enlarging complex cyst
- Large cyst with torsion risk or mass effect
- Suspicious malignant features
- Severe symptoms despite conservative management
- Fertility planning when endometrioma affects access, pain, or ovarian reserve decisions

Preferred approach for benign disease is cystectomy with ovarian preservation when feasible. Oophorectomy may be required for nonviable torsed ovary, suspicious malignancy, or cysts replacing ovarian tissue.

## Acute Complications

### Ovarian Torsion

Torsion is a surgical emergency. Suspect with sudden unilateral pelvic pain, nausea/vomiting, adnexal enlargement, or intermittent colicky pain. Doppler flow does not exclude torsion because dual ovarian blood supply can persist.

### Ruptured or Hemorrhagic Cyst

Presentation ranges from mild self-limited pain to hemoperitoneum and shock. Management depends on stability:

- Stable: analgesia, observation, repeat exam/imaging as needed
- Unstable: resuscitation and urgent laparoscopy/laparotomy

### Tubo-Ovarian Abscess

Complex adnexal mass with fever, leukocytosis, cervical motion tenderness, and PID risk requires broad-spectrum antibiotics and possible drainage/surgery if large, ruptured, or not improving.

## Pregnancy Considerations

- Corpus luteum cysts are common in early pregnancy and typically resolve.
- Persistent, large, symptomatic, or complex masses require specialist evaluation.
- Surgery, if needed and non-emergent, is often planned in the second trimester.
- Acute torsion or rupture is managed urgently regardless of gestational age.

## Management Algorithm

```text
Adnexal cyst/mass on ultrasound
        |
        v
Pregnancy test + assess stability
        |
        +--> unstable / torsion concern / acute abdomen -> urgent surgical evaluation
        |
        v
Ultrasound morphology
        |
        +--> simple, premenopausal, asymptomatic -> expectant management +/- interval US
        |
        +--> hemorrhagic/functional likely -> analgesia, repeat US 6-12 weeks if needed
        |
        +--> endometrioma/dermoid classic -> counsel, surveillance vs cystectomy based on symptoms/size/fertility
        |
        +--> solid/papillary/ascites/postmenopausal concerning -> tumor markers + gyn oncology referral
```
