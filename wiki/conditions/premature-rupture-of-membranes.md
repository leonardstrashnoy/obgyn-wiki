## Clinical Definition

Premature rupture of membranes (PROM) is rupture of the amniotic sac before the onset of labor. **Preterm PROM (PPROM)** is ROM before 37 weeks. Prolonged ROM is > 18–24 hours before delivery (1A).

## Epidemiology

- PPROM occurs in 2–4% of all pregnancies
- Accounts for 30–40% of preterm births
- Chorioamnionitis complicates 15–25% of PPROM cases

## Risk Factors

- Prior PPROM or preterm birth
- Short cervical length
- Genital tract infection (bacterial vaginosis, STIs)
- Smoking, bleeding, multiple gestation
- Amniocentesis / invasive procedures

## Management

**Preterm PPROM (< 37 weeks):**
- Expectant management if no infection/fetal compromise
- **Antibiotics prolong latency** (1A — erythromycin or ampicillin/amoxicillin)
- **Antenatal corticosteroids** if < 34 weeks (1A)
- **Magnesium sulfate** neuroprotection if < 32 weeks anticipated (1A)
- Delivery if chorioamnionitis, nonreassuring testing, or labor

**Term PROM (≥ 37 weeks):**
- Induction of labor (reduces chorioamnionitis without increasing cesarean) (1A)
