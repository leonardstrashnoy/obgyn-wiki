## Clinical Definition

Multiple pregnancy is a pregnancy with more than one fetus (twins, triplets, or higher-order multiples). Dizygotic (fraternal) twins result from multiple ovulation; monozygotic (identical) twins result from splitting of a single embryo (1A).

## Epidemiology

- Twins: ~1 in 80 spontaneous pregnancies (higher with ART)
- Triplets: ~1 in 6400
- Monozygotic twins: 3–4 per 1000 births, constant worldwide

## Complications

- Preterm birth (60% of twins before 37 weeks)
- Fetal growth restriction
- Preeclampsia (2–3× risk)
- Gestational diabetes
- Placental abruption
- Twin-to-twin transfusion syndrome (monochorionic)
- Higher perinatal mortality vs singletons

## Management

- Increased surveillance (every 2 weeks from 20 weeks for monochorionic)
- Ultrasound for chorionicity (first trimester critical)
- Cervical length screening
- Delivery planning: twins at 38 weeks (dichorionic), 36–37 weeks (monochorionic)
- Tertiary referral for TTTS, sIUGR, TRAP
