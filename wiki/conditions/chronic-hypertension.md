## Clinical Definition

Chronic hypertension is BP ≥140/90 mmHg present before pregnancy or before 20 weeks gestation, or persisting >12 weeks postpartum (1B).

## Risks

- Superimposed preeclampsia (25%)
- Placental abruption
- FGR / IUGR
- Preterm birth
- Stroke, heart failure (severe)

## Management

- **Target BP:** < 140/90 (not < 120/80 — avoid fetal hypoperfusion)
- **First-line agents:** Labetalol, nifedipine, methyldopa (1B)
- Avoid ACE inhibitors, ARBs, direct renin inhibitors (teratogenic)
- Low-dose aspirin for superimposed preeclampsia prevention (1B)

## Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence Level | Basis |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Labetalol / nifedipine / methyldopa first-line | 1B | ACOG + RCTs |
| ACE inhibitors teratogenic | 1B | Cohort + teratology |
| Superimposed preeclampsia in ~25% | 2B | Cohort data |
| Low-dose aspirin reduces superimposed PE | 1B | RCT meta-analysis |
