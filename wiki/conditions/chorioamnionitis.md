## Clinical Definition

Chorioamnionitis (also termed intra-amniotic infection) is an acute inflammation of the fetal membranes (chorion and amnion) typically due to ascending bacterial infection from the lower genital tract (2B). It represents a major cause of maternal and neonatal morbidity, and is strongly associated with preterm labor and delivery.

## Types and Classification

- **Clinical chorioamnionitis**: Maternal fever (≥38°C) plus at least two of: maternal/fetal tachycardia, uterine tenderness, purulent amniotic fluid, or leukocytosis (2B)
- **Histologic chorioamnionitis**: Inflammation on placental histopathology; may be present without clinical signs (2B)
- **Subclinical (occult) infection**: Documented by amniocentesis culture/PCR positive without maternal fever; increasingly recognized driver of preterm labor (2B)

## Etiology and Risk Factors

### Major Risk Factors (2B)
- **Prolonged rupture of membranes (PROM)** > 18 hours (strongest predictor)
- **Preterm premature rupture of membranes (PPROM)**
- Multiple vaginal examinations during labor
- Prolonged labor
- Nulliparity
- Internal fetal monitoring
- Meconium-stained amniotic fluid
- Bacterial vaginosis and genital tract colonization (Gardnerella, Ureaplasma, Mycoplasma)

### Microbiology (2B)
- Most commonly polymicrobial
- **Ascending route**: Escherichia coli, Group B Streptococcus (GBS), Enterococcus, Ureaplasma species, Mycoplasma hominis
- **Hematogenous route**: Listeria monocytogenes (rare, more often causes placental villitis)

## Pathophysiology

### Inflammatory Cascade (2B)
1. Bacterial colonization of amniotic cavity triggers innate immune response
2. **Pattern recognition receptors (TLR-2, TLR-4)** on trophoblasts recognize pathogen-associated molecular patterns
3. Release of **pro-inflammatory cytokines**: IL-1β, IL-6, IL-8, TNF-α
4. **Prostaglandin synthesis** stimulated → uterine contractility and cervical ripening
5. **Matrix metalloproteinase (MMP)** activation → membrane degradation and rupture
6. **Neutrophil chemotaxis** to chorion and amnion → histologic chorioamnionitis

### Fetal Inflammatory Response (2B)
- Fetal monocytes produce IL-6 ("fetal inflammatory response syndrome")
- Associated with **white matter injury** and **cerebral palsy** in preterm neonates
- Fetal bacteremia and sepsis in severe cases

## Clinical Features and Diagnosis

### Maternal Signs (2B)
- Fever ≥ 38°C (most common)
- Maternal tachycardia (>100 bpm)
- Uterine tenderness/fundal tenderness
- Foul-smelling or purulent amniotic fluid
- Leukocytosis (>15,000/mm³)

### Fetal Signs (2B)
- Fetal tachycardia (>160 bpm)
- Decreased variability on fetal heart rate tracing
- Late decelerations

### Laboratory Confirmation
- **Amniocentesis with Gram stain, culture, glucose** (gold standard but invasive)
- **Biomarkers**: maternal serum IL-6, CRP, procalcitonin (supportive, not diagnostic)

## Complications

### Maternal (2B)
- **Postpartum hemorrhage** (uterine atony due to infection)
- **Postpartum endometritis**
- Sepsis (rare)
- Wound infection after cesarean delivery

### Fetal/Neonatal (2B)
- **Preterm birth** (most common outcome; often iatrogenic or spontaneous)
- **Early-onset neonatal sepsis**
- Pneumonia
- Meningitis
- **Cerebral palsy** (associated with fetal inflammatory response in preterm infants)
- Periventricular leukomalacia

## Management

### Intrapartum (2B)
- **Broad-spectrum intravenous antibiotics** once diagnosed:
  - Ampicillin + Gentamicin (standard)
  - Add anaerobic coverage (Clindamycin/Metronidazole) if cesarean delivery planned
- Expedited delivery typically indicated once chorioamnionitis is diagnosed in labor
- Continuous fetal heart rate monitoring

### Neonatal (2B)
- Sepsis evaluation and empiric antibiotics for all neonates born to mothers with chorioamnionitis
- Supportive care as indicated

## Prevention

- **Prophylactic antibiotics** for PPROM (reduces latency and chorioamnionitis risk) (1A)
- Minimize unnecessary vaginal examinations
- Treatment of bacterial vaginosis in pregnancy (contested benefit)
- Group B Streptococcus screening and intrapartum prophylaxis (1A)

## Related Conditions

- **Preterm labor**: chorioamnionitis is a leading identifiable cause (2B)
- **Preterm premature rupture of membranes (PPROM)**: strong bidirectional relationship (2B)
- **Cervical incompetence**: ascending infection risk increased with cervical shortening (2B)
- **Postpartum hemorrhage**: uterine atony worsened by intra-amniotic infection (2B)

## Prognosis

Most cases respond promptly to antibiotics and delivery with full maternal recovery. Neonatal outcomes depend heavily on **gestational age at delivery** and **severity of fetal inflammatory response**. Preterm neonates exposed to chorioamnionitis have elevated risks of neurodevelopmental impairment even without culture-proven sepsis (2B).

## Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence Level | Basis |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Maternal fever ≥38°C + ≥2 criteria = clinical chorioamnionitis | 2B | Consensus definition from multiple cohort studies |

| Ampicillin + gentamicin standard intrapartum regimen | 2B | Observational cohorts + expert guidelines |
