## Clinical Definition

Postterm pregnancy is pregnancy beyond 42 weeks 0 days gestation. Prolonged pregnancy is 41 weeks 0 days to 41 weeks 6 days. Post-dates is ≥ 41 weeks (1A).

## Risks

- Stillbirth increases after 41 weeks
- Oligohydramnios
- Macrosomia / shoulder dystocia
- Meconium aspiration
- Dysfunctional labor / cesarean

## Management

- **Antenatal surveillance:** NST or BPP twice weekly from 41 weeks (2B)
- **Induction of labor:** Recommended at 41 weeks; reduces perinatal mortality without increasing cesarean (1A — SWEPIS, INDEX trials)
- Prior belief that expectant management to 42 weeks was safe has been overturned by recent RCTs

## Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence Level | Basis |
|-------|-------------|-------|
