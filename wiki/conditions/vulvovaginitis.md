# Vulvovaginitis

**Vulvovaginitis** is inflammation or irritation of the vulva and vagina presenting with discharge, odor, itch, burning, dysuria, dyspareunia, or vulvar pain. The common infectious causes are bacterial vaginosis, vulvovaginal candidiasis, and trichomoniasis; noninfectious causes include irritant dermatitis, atrophy, inflammatory vaginitis, and dermatologic disease.

## Differential Diagnosis

| Pattern | Suggestive Features |
|---|---|
| Bacterial vaginosis | Thin gray discharge, fishy odor, pH >4.5 |
| Candidiasis | Pruritus, vulvar erythema, thick discharge, normal pH |
| Trichomoniasis | Frothy discharge, odor, pH >4.5, STI risk |
| Cervicitis | Mucopurulent discharge, postcoital bleeding, cervical friability |
| Atrophic / GSM | Dryness, burning, dyspareunia, postmenopause/lactation |
| Contact dermatitis | External irritation, new products, pads, soaps, topical agents |

## Evaluation

- History: discharge, odor, itch, pain, bleeding, sexual exposure, pregnancy, diabetes, antibiotics, irritants.
- Exam: vulvar skin, discharge, vaginal/cervical inflammation, lesions, foreign body.
- Point-of-care tests when available: vaginal pH, amine/whiff test, saline microscopy, KOH microscopy.
- NAAT testing is preferred when trichomoniasis or cervicitis pathogens are suspected or microscopy is unavailable.
- Avoid diagnosing based on symptoms alone because overlap is substantial.

## Initial Management Principles

1. Treat the confirmed cause when possible.
2. Test for STIs when cervicitis, trichomoniasis risk, pelvic pain, pregnancy, or high-risk exposure is present.
3. Avoid repeated empiric antifungal therapy without confirmation in recurrent symptoms.
4. Consider noninfectious etiologies when testing is negative or symptoms persist.
5. In pregnancy, use pregnancy-compatible regimens and evaluate symptomatic infections promptly.

## Recurrent Symptoms

Recurrent vulvovaginal symptoms require reassessment rather than automatic retreatment. Consider resistant yeast species, recurrent BV, dermatologic disease, vulvodynia, genitourinary syndrome of menopause, and exposure-related dermatitis.
