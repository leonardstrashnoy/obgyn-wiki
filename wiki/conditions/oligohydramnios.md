## Clinical Definition

Oligohydramnios is abnormally low amniotic fluid volume, defined as AFI ≤ 5 cm or DVP < 2 cm (2B). Post-term pregnancy is the most common cause.

## Etiology

- Post-term pregnancy (placental senescence)
- Placental insufficiency / FGR
- Rupture of membranes
- Fetal renal anomalies (agenesis, obstruction)
- Medications (ACE inhibitors, NSAIDs)

## Complications

- Fetal pulmonary hypoplasia (if early onset)
- Potter sequence (compression deformities)
- Cord compression → nonreassuring FHR
- Increased cesarean for fetal intolerance

## Management

- Identify etiology (anatomy scan, Doppler, ROM check)
- Delivery if post-term or FGR with abnormal testing
- Amnioinfusion intrapartum for variable decelerations
- Maternal hydration may transiently increase AFI
