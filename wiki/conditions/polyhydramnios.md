## Clinical Definition

Polyhydramnios is abnormally increased amniotic fluid volume, typically defined as AFI ≥ 24 cm or DVP ≥ 8 cm. Idiopathic in 50–60% of cases (2B).

## Etiology

- Fetal GI obstruction (esophageal atresia, duodenal atresia)
- Neural tube defects (anencephaly — impaired swallowing)
- Twin-to-twin transfusion syndrome
- Maternal diabetes mellitus
- Fetal anemia / hydrops
- Idiopathic (most common)

## Complications

- Preterm labor / PPROM
- Cord prolapse at membrane rupture
- Placental abruption
- Postpartum hemorrhage (uterine atony)
- Maternal dyspnea

## Management

- Targeted fetal anatomy survey, diabetes screening, fetal echocardiography
- Therapeutic amniocentesis for maternal compromise
- Indomethacin (reduces fetal urine) — limited to <32 weeks due to ductus constriction risk
- Delivery at term
