## Clinical Definition

Chlamydia trachomatis is the most common bacterial sexually transmitted infection. Asymptomatic in ~70% of women, it is a leading cause of PID, tubal factor infertility, and ectopic pregnancy (1A).

## Screening

- **USPSTF / ACOG:** Annual screening for all sexually active women < 25, and older women with risk factors (1A)
- NAAT from urine or vaginal/cervical swab

## Treatment

- **First-line:** Doxycycline 100 mg BID × 7 days (1A)
- **Alternative:** Azithromycin 1 g single dose (1A) — preferred in pregnancy
- Test of cure at 4 weeks (especially if pregnant or azithromycin used)
- Partner treatment essential

## Complications

- PID, tubo-ovarian abscess
- Tubal factor infertility
- Ectopic pregnancy
- Chronic pelvic pain
- Fitz-Hugh-Curtis syndrome (perihepatitis)
