## Clinical Definition

Early-onset neonatal sepsis (EONS) is bacterial infection occurring within 72 hours of birth, typically vertically transmitted from mother. Group B Streptococcus (GBS) and E. coli are the predominant pathogens (1A).

## Epidemiology

- Incidence: 0.5–1 per 1000 live births in developed countries
- Mortality: 5–15% despite antibiotics
- GBS: ~40% of cases; E. coli: ~30% (increasing, especially in preterm)

## Risk Factors

- Maternal chorioamnionitis
- GBS colonization without intrapartum prophylaxis
- Preterm birth
- Prolonged ROM (>18 hours)
- Multiple gestation

## Prevention

- **GBS universal screening** at 35–37 weeks (vaginal-rectal culture)
- **Intrapartum antibiotic prophylaxis (IAP):** Penicillin G or ampicillin for GBS-positive or unknown status with risk factors
- IAP reduces GBS EONS by ~80% (1A)

## Management

- Empiric ampicillin + gentamicin for suspected EONS
- Adjust based on cultures and local resistance
- Supportive care; consider lumbar puncture if meningitis suspected
