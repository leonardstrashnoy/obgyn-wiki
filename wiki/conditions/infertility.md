## Clinical Definition

Infertility is defined as failure to achieve pregnancy after 12 months of regular unprotected intercourse (6 months if female partner > 35 years). Affects ~10–15% of couples globally (1A).

## Etiology

**Female factors (~50%):**
- Ovulatory dysfunction (PCOS, hypothalamic amenorrhea, ovarian failure)
- Tubal factor (prior PID, ectopic pregnancy, surgery)
- Uterine/cervical factor (fibroids, polyps, adhesions)
- Endometriosis

**Male factors (~30%):**
- Oligospermia, asthenospermia, teratospermia
- Varicocele, cryptorchidism, genetic (Klinefelter)

**Combined / unexplained (~20%)**

## Evaluation

- Ovarian reserve (AMH, FSH, AFC)
- HSG or SIS for tubal patency / uterine cavity
- Semen analysis
- Ovulation tracking

## Treatment

- Ovulation induction (clomiphene, letrozole, gonadotropins)
- Intrauterine insemination (IUI)
- In vitro fertilization (IVF)
- Surgery for tubal / uterine pathology

## Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence Level | Basis |
