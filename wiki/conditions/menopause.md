# Menopause

**Menopause** is defined as the permanent cessation of menstruation resulting from loss of ovarian follicular activity. It is diagnosed retrospectively after 12 consecutive months of amenorrhea not explained by other causes.

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Menopause** | Final menstrual period; 12 months amenorrhea |
| **Perimenopause** | Transition period with menstrual cycle variability; begins ~4 years before final menses |
| **Postmenopause** | Time from final menstrual period onward |
| **Premature menopause** | Menopause <40 years (1% of women) |
| **Early menopause** | Menopause 40-45 years (5-10% of women) |

## Physiology

### Hormonal Changes
- Declining ovarian follicular reserve
- Loss of negative feedback → ↑ FSH, ↑ LH
- ↓ Estradiol (primary estrogen before menopause)
- ↓ Inhibin B
- ↑ FSH >40 IU/L diagnostic (but not required)

### After Menopause
- **Estrone** becomes primary estrogen (from peripheral conversion of androstenedione in adipose)
- Minimal progesterone production
- Androgens persist (ovarian and adrenal)

## Clinical Features

### Vasomotor Symptoms (VMS)
- **Hot flashes/night sweats**: 75-80% of women
- Duration: Average 7.4 years, can persist >10 years
- Frequency: 1-2 per hour to several per day
- Severity: Varies widely

### Genitourinary Syndrome of Menopause (GSM)
- Vulvovaginal atrophy (dryness, irritation, dyspareunia)
- Urinary symptoms (urgency, frequency, dysuria, recurrent UTIs)
- Mechanism: Estrogen deficiency → tissue thinning, pH elevation

### Other Symptoms
- Sleep disturbance (often from night sweats)
- Mood changes (anxiety, depression, irritability)
- Cognitive symptoms ("brain fog")
- Joint stiffness and pain
- Skin and hair changes

### Long-term Health Consequences

| Condition | Mechanism | Risk |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| **Osteoporosis** | Estrogen ↓ bone resorption | Risk ↑ 2-3x |
| **Cardiovascular disease** | Loss of cardioprotection | Leading cause of death postmenopause |
| **Cognitive decline** | Estrogen role in neural function | Variable evidence |

## Diagnosis

### Clinical
- **Age 45-55 + classic symptoms**: Clinical diagnosis
- **Age <45**: FSH and estradiol to confirm (amenorrhea + FSH >40 IU/L)

### Differential for Secondary Amenorrhea
- Pregnancy (always exclude)
- Thyroid dysfunction
- Hyperprolactinemia
- Hypothalamic amenorrhea
- Premature ovarian insufficiency

## Management

### Menopausal Hormone Therapy (MHT)

**Systemic estrogen** (Level: 1A)
- Most effective treatment for vasomotor symptoms
- Also protects bone
- Routes: Oral, transdermal patch/gel, vaginal (inadequate for systemic symptoms)

**Progestogen requirement**: 
- **Intact uterus**: Must add progestogen (prevents endometrial hyperplasia/cancer)
- **Hysterectomy**: Estrogen alone sufficient

**Regimens**:
- Cyclic: Monthly withdrawal bleeding
- Continuous combined: No bleeding (preferred if postmenopausal)
- Transdermal estradiol: Preferred for >60 years or cardiovascular risk (↓ VTE, stroke risk)

**Duration**: Individualized; reassess at 3-5 years. Lowest effective dose, shortest duration consistent with goals.

### Contraindications to MHT (Absolute)

- Known breast cancer
- Known or suspected estrogen-dependent neoplasia
- Undiagnosed abnormal genital bleeding
- Untreated endometrial hyperplasia
- History of venous thromboembolism (VTE) or stroke
- Active liver disease
- Pregnancy

### Non-Hormonal Options

**SSRI/SNRI** (Level: 1A for VMS)
- Paroxetine 7.5 mg, venlafaxine 75 mg
- 50-60% reduction in hot flash frequency
- Good for women with breast cancer (tamoxifen interaction consideration)

**Gabapentin** (Level: 2A)
- 900 mg divide dose effective for VMS
- Sedating; useful if insomnia prominent

**Clonidine** (Level: 3)
- Modest effect; limited by side effects

**Cognitive behavioral therapy** (Level: 2A)
- Effective for VMS and sleep

### Genitourinary Syndrome

**Low-dose vaginal estrogen** (Level: 1A)
- Minimal systemic absorption
- No progestogen requirement
- Forms: Cream, tablet, ring
- Very effective for local symptoms (90% improvement)

**Non-hormonal**:
- Vaginal moisturizers (regular use)
- Lubricants (sexual activity)
- Ospemifene (SERM) — oral option

### Osteoporosis Prevention

**First-line**
- Calcium (1200 mg/day) and vitamin D (800-1000 IU/day)
- Weight-bearing exercise
- Smoking cessation
- Limit alcohol

**Pharmacologic** (if T-score <-2.5 or high FRAX risk)
- **Bisphosphonates** (alendronate, risedronate): First-line
- **Denosumab**: Alternative; requires sequential bisphosphonate on discontinuation

## Evidence Summary

| Intervention | Indication | Evidence Level |
|--------------|------------|----------------|
| Systemic MHT | Vasomotor symptoms | 1A |

| SSRI/SNRI | VMS (breast cancer) | 1A |
| Vaginal estrogen | GSM | 1A |
| Bisphosphonates | Osteoporosis | 1A |
