## Clinical Definition

Shoulder dystocia is an obstetric emergency where the fetal anterior shoulder impacts on the maternal symphysis pubis after delivery of the head, preventing spontaneous delivery of the body (2B).

## Risk Factors

- Prior shoulder dystocia (10–20% recurrence)
- Macrosomia (especially estimated > 4500 g in diabetic, > 5000 g non-diabetic)
- Maternal diabetes, obesity
- Prolonged second stage, operative vaginal delivery
- Postterm pregnancy

## Management (HELPERR Mnemonic)

- **H**elp — call anesthesia, pediatrics, additional staff
- **E**valuate for episiotomy (if needed for maneuvers)
- **L**egs — McRoberts maneuver (hyperflexion, abduction)
- **P**ressure — suprapubic pressure (not fundal)
- **E**nter — Wood's screw or reverse Wood's screw (internal rotation)
- **R**emove — posterior arm delivery
- **R**oll — all-fours position (Gaskin maneuver)

## Complications

- **Brachial plexus injury** (Erb's palsy): 10–20% of cases; most resolve
- Fractured clavicle/humerus (usually heal well)
- Hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy (if delay > 5–7 minutes)
- Fetal death (rare)
- Maternal: PPH, 3rd/4th degree tears, symphyseal separation

## Prevention

- Difficult to predict or prevent; most cases have no risk factors
- Prophylactic cesarean for estimated weight > 5000 g (non-diabetic) or > 4500 g (diabetic) may be considered (2B)
- Macrosomia induction not clearly preventive
