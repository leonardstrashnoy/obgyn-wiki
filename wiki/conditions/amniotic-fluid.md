## Clinical Definition

Amniotic fluid volume (AFV) disorders encompass abnormalities in the amount of fluid surrounding the fetus. **Oligohydramnios** is defined as abnormally low AFV, typically AFI ≤ 5 cm or deepest vertical pocket (DVP) < 2 cm (2B). **Polyhydramnios** is abnormally elevated AFV, typically AFI ≥ 24 cm or DVP ≥ 8 cm (2B). Normal AFV increases progressively from ~100 mL at 12 weeks to 800–1000 mL by term, then declines slightly post-dates.

## Epidemiology

- **Oligohydramnios:** Occurs in 0.5–8% of pregnancies depending on definition and gestational age (2B)
- **Polyhydramnios:** Occurs in 0.2–1.6% of pregnancies; idiopathic in 50–60% of cases (2B)
- Both conditions are associated with increased perinatal morbidity and mortality (2B)

## Risk Factors

### Oligohydramnios (2B)
- Post-term pregnancy (>41 weeks)
- Placental insufficiency / IUGR
- Rupture of membranes (ROM)
- Fetal renal anomalies (bilateral renal agenesis, obstructive uropathy)
- Fetal growth restriction
- Maternal dehydration
- Medications (ACE inhibitors, NSAIDs in third trimester)

### Polyhydramnios (2B)
- Fetal GI obstruction (esophageal atresia, duodenal atresia)
- Neural tube defects (anencephaly)
- Twin-to-twin transfusion syndrome
- Maternal diabetes mellitus
- Fetal anemia / hydrops
- Idiopathic (most common)

## Pathophysiology

Normal AFV is maintained by a balance of production and removal (2B):

**Production:**
- Fetal urine (primary source after mid-gestation): 200–1200 mL/day
- Fetal lung fluid secretion: ~100–200 mL/day net contribution
- Intramembranous pathway transfers fluid from fetal circulation

**Removal:**
- Fetal swallowing: 200–750 mL/day
- Intramembranous absorption: 200–500 mL/day

**Oligohydramnios mechanisms:**
- Decreased fetal urine output (renal agenesis, placental insufficiency)
- Chronic membrane leak
- Impaired intramembranous transport

**Polyhydramnios mechanisms:**
- Decreased fetal swallowing (GI obstruction, CNS anomalies)
- Increased fetal urine output (diabetes, twin transfusion)
- Increased placental/fetal transudation

## Diagnosis

### Ultrasound Assessment (2B)

**Amniotic Fluid Index (AFI):**
- Sum of deepest vertical pockets in four quadrants
- Normal: 8–24 cm
- Oligohydramnios: ≤ 5 cm (some use ≤ 8 cm as mild)
- Polyhydramnios: ≥ 24 cm (some use ≥ 20 cm as mild)

**Deepest Vertical Pocket (DVP):**
- Single largest vertical pocket
- Normal: 2–8 cm
- Oligohydramnios: < 2 cm
- Polyhydramnios: ≥ 8 cm

**Technical considerations (2B):**
- Exclude umbilical cord and fetal limbs from measurement
- Measure at 90° to uterine contour
- Lower frequency transducer may help in obese patients (adipose tissue scatters beam)
- Maternal pressure on abdomen can reduce AFI by ~21%
- Inter-observer variability exists; consistent technique required

## Evidence-based Management

### Oligohydramnios (2B)
- **Identify etiology:** ROM check, fetal anatomy scan, growth assessment, Doppler studies
- **Expectant management:** If isolated, near term, and fetal testing reassuring
- **Delivery timing:** Consider delivery at 36–37 weeks if persistent and unexplained
- **Amnioinfusion:** Intrapartum for variable decelerations (2B benefit for short-term neonatal outcomes)
- **Maternal hydration:** Oral or IV hydration may transiently increase AFI in dehydrated patients

### Polyhydramnios (2B)
- **Identify etiology:** Targeted fetal anatomy survey, diabetes screening, fetal echocardiography
- **Expectant management:** For mild idiopathic cases with reassuring fetal status
- **Therapeutic amniocentesis:** For maternal respiratory compromise or preterm labor risk
- **Indomethacin:** Reduces fetal urine output; use limited to <32 weeks due to fetal ductus arteriosus constriction risk (2B)
- **Delivery timing:** Usually term; earlier if maternal compromise or severe symptomatic cases

## Complications and Prognosis

### Oligohydramnios (2B)
- Fetal pulmonary hypoplasia (if early onset / prolonged)
- Fetal compression deformities (Potter sequence)
- Umbilical cord compression
- Nonreassuring fetal heart rate patterns in labor
- Increased cesarean delivery rate for fetal intolerance

### Polyhydramnios (2B)
- Preterm labor / PPROM
- Cord prolapse at membrane rupture
- Placental abruption
- Postpartum hemorrhage (uterine atony)
- Maternal dyspnea / respiratory compromise

## Differential Diagnosis

- Normal physiologic variation
- Measurement error (technique, maternal habitus)
- Fetal anomaly (renal, GI, CNS)
- Placental dysfunction
- Multiple gestation
- Maternal metabolic disorder

## Active Research / Open Questions

- Optimal definition thresholds for AFI vs DVP across gestational ages
- Role of dynamic fluid assessment vs single measurement
- Intramembranous pathway quantification in human pregnancy
- Long-term outcomes for idiopathic mild polyhydramnios
