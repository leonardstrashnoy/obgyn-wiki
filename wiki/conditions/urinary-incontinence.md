# Urinary Incontinence

**Urinary incontinence (UI)** is involuntary urine leakage. It is common, underreported, and treatable. Initial management is usually clinical and conservative, with escalation based on UI subtype, bother, goals, comorbidity, and findings such as hematuria, recurrent infection, retention, or pelvic organ prolapse.

## Classification

| Type | Typical Symptom Pattern | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Stress UI | Leakage with cough, laugh, exercise, lifting | Urethral hypermobility or sphincter deficiency |
| Urgency UI | Sudden urge followed by leakage | Detrusor overactivity / bladder sensory urgency |
| Mixed UI | Both stress and urgency symptoms | Combined mechanisms |
| Overflow | Continuous dribbling, high residual | Retention / impaired emptying |
| Functional | Leakage due to mobility/cognition barriers | Access-to-toilet limitation |

## Initial Evaluation

- History: leakage triggers, frequency, nocturia, urgency, pad use, fluid/caffeine, medications, obstetric and surgical history.
- Screen for red flags: hematuria, pelvic mass, recurrent UTIs, neurologic symptoms, fistula concern, new retention.
- Physical exam: pelvic exam, cough stress test with comfortably full bladder, atrophy, prolapse, pelvic floor contraction.
- Urinalysis to exclude infection or hematuria.
- Postvoid residual when retention, voiding dysfunction, neurologic disease, prior surgery, or overflow is suspected.
- Bladder diary helps quantify frequency, volume, urgency, and triggers.

## First-Line Management

### Lifestyle and Behavioral Therapy

- Fluid optimization; avoid excessive restriction.
- Reduce bladder irritants if clearly associated (caffeine, carbonated beverages, alcohol).
- Weight loss improves stress and urgency UI in patients with obesity.
- Timed voiding and bladder training for urgency UI.
- Constipation treatment and smoking cessation support.

### Pelvic Floor Muscle Training

Pelvic floor muscle training is first-line for stress and mixed UI. Supervised programs are more effective than unsupervised advice alone. Typical course: at least 3 months with progressive contraction/relaxation training and functional bracing before leakage triggers.

## Subtype-Specific Treatment

### Stress Urinary Incontinence

- First-line: pelvic floor muscle training, pessary or continence device for selected patients.
- Surgical options for persistent bothersome SUI include midurethral sling, autologous fascial sling, Burch colposuspension, or urethral bulking.
- Counsel about mesh-specific risks, retention, pain, urgency symptoms, and future pregnancy considerations.

### Urgency Urinary Incontinence / Overactive Bladder

- First-line: bladder training and behavioral changes.
- Medications: antimuscarinics or beta-3 agonists after counseling about dry mouth, constipation, cognitive risk in older patients, hypertension considerations, and drug interactions.
- Third-line: posterior tibial nerve stimulation, sacral neuromodulation, or intradetrusor botulinum toxin for refractory symptoms.

### Mixed UI

Treat the dominant/bothersome component first. Reassess after conservative therapy; untreated urgency can persist after stress-incontinence surgery.

## Referral / Urodynamics

Refer to urogynecology/urology when diagnosis is uncertain, prior incontinence surgery failed, significant prolapse coexists, voiding dysfunction or high residual is present, neurologic disease is suspected, hematuria is unexplained, or invasive treatment is planned and symptoms are complex.
