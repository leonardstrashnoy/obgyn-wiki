## Clinical Definition

Preterm birth is delivery before 37 weeks 0 days gestation. It is subdivided into:
- **Late preterm:** 34⁰⁄₇ to 36⁶⁄₇ weeks
- **Moderate preterm:** 32⁰⁄₇ to 33⁶⁄₇ weeks
- **Very preterm:** 28⁰⁄₇ to 31⁶⁄₇ weeks
- **Extremely preterm:** < 28 weeks (1A)

## Epidemiology

- Globally: ~11% of all live births (range 5–18% by region)
- Leading cause of neonatal death and long-term disability
- Incidence increasing in many developed countries due to ART, multiple gestation, and advanced maternal age

## Risk Factors

- Prior preterm birth (strongest predictor)
- Short cervical length (< 25 mm)
- Multiple gestation
- Uterine anomalies, cervical surgery (cone biopsy)
- Infections (UTI, periodontal disease, vaginal infections)
- PPROM
- Medical conditions (preeclampsia requiring early delivery, diabetes, SLE)
- Lifestyle: smoking, cocaine, stress, low BMI

## Prevention

- **Progesterone:** Vaginal progesterone for TVCL 10–20 mm; 17-OHPC IM for prior singleton PTB (1B)
- **Cerclage:** For TVCL < 25 mm + prior PTB in singleton (1B)
- **Smoking cessation, infection screening/treatment**
- **Reduced multifetal pregnancies** (elective single embryo transfer)

## Management

- **Antenatal corticosteroids** before 34 weeks (1A — reduces RDS, IVH, NEC mortality)
- **Magnesium sulfate** neuroprotection if < 32 weeks anticipated (1A)
- **Tocolysis:** Short-term 48-hour window for steroids transfer (nifedipine, indomethacin, magnesium) (1B)
- **Antibiotics for PPROM:** Prolong latency, reduce chorioamnionitis (1A)
- **GBS prophylaxis** if preterm and unknown status
