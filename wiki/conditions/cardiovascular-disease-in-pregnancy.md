## Clinical Definition

Cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of maternal mortality in developed countries. Includes congenital heart disease, cardiomyopathy, valvular disease, and acquired conditions (2B).

## Risk Stratification

- **Modified WHO Class I–II:** Low risk — can usually proceed with pregnancy
- **WHO Class III:** Significantly increased risk — requires counseling
- **WHO Class IV:** Extremely high risk — pregnancy contraindicated

## Management

- Preconception counseling and cardiac optimization
- Multidisciplinary team (cardiology, MFM, anesthesia, cardiac surgery)
- Delivery planning based on functional status
- Vaginal delivery preferred in most cases
