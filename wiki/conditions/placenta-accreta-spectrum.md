## Clinical Definition

Placenta accreta spectrum (PAS) describes abnormal placental adherence/invasion: **accreta** (attachment to myometrium without intervening decidua), **increta** (invasion into myometrium), **percreta** (invasion through serosa or into adjacent organs) (2B).

## Risk Factors

- **Prior cesarean delivery:** Risk increases with number (0.3% after 1, 6.7% after 5+)
- **Placenta previa:** Strongest single risk factor
- **Prior uterine surgery:** Myomectomy, curettage, Asherman syndrome
- **Advanced maternal age, IVF, smoking**

## Diagnosis

- **Ultrasound (18–32 weeks):** Loss of retroplacental clear space, multiple lacunae, bladder line interruption
- **MRI:** For posterior placenta or suspected percreta; assesses bladder / bowel invasion

## Management

- **Planned prelabor cesarean hysterectomy** at 34–35 weeks with multidisciplinary team (2B)
- **Conservative management:** Fertility-sparing; higher hemorrhage / ICU / reoperation risk; reserved for selected cases
- **Uterine artery embolization, balloon catheters** as adjuncts
- **Cell salvage, massive transfusion protocol** preparation

## Complications

- Massive hemorrhage (average 2–5 L)
- Hysterectomy (often unavoidable)
- Bladder / bowel injury
- DIC
- Maternal death (rare with planned care; high with emergency)
